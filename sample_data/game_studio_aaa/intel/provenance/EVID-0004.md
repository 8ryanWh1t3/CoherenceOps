## 1. Summary

The "Luminary-V3" churn elasticity model, used to forecast player churn in response
to MTX price changes, **failed validation** against W11 EU price-increase data.
The model predicted 3.0% churn (+/- 1.2%) for a 10% price increase; actual observed
churn was **8.2%** -- a 2.7x underprediction. The model's R-squared degraded from
0.94 on training data to **0.71** on the W11 validation set, indicating structural
model error rather than noise.

## 2. Model Specification

### 2.1 Training Configuration

| Parameter                | Value                                          |

| Model type               | Ridge regression with interaction terms        |
| Training period           | Season 1 W01 -- Season 2 W20 (40 weeks)      |
| Dependent variable        | 14-day churn rate (no login within 14d)       |
| Independent variables     | Price delta (%), region, tenure bucket, spend quintile |
| Regularization (alpha)    | 0.1                                           |
| Training R-squared        | 0.94                                          |
| Training RMSE             | 0.8pp                                         |
| Cross-validation (5-fold) | R-squared 0.91 (+/- 0.03)                    |

| Region                         | EU                        |

## 3. Actual vs. Predicted Results

### 3.1 Overall

| Metric                | Predicted      | Actual   | Delta     |
|-----------------------|---------------|----------|-----------|
| 14-day churn rate     | 3.0%          | 8.2%     | +5.2pp    |
| Revenue impact (EU)   | -$1.1M/month  | -$3.8M/month | +$2.7M |
| Refund rate           | 1.4%          | 6.8%     | +5.4pp    |

### 3.2 Churn by Tenure Bucket

| Tenure         | Predicted Churn | Actual Churn | Delta   |
|----------------|----------------|-------------|---------|
| < 30 days      | 5.1%           | 14.3%       | +9.2pp  |
| 30--90 days    | 3.4%           | 9.8%        | +6.4pp  |
| 90--180 days   | 2.2%           | 6.1%        | +3.9pp  |

**Key finding:** Newer players (< 90 days) churned at dramatically higher rates
than predicted. The model's training data was dominated by Season 1 veterans who
had higher sunk-cost tolerance.

## 4. Diagnostic Analysis

### 4.1 R-Squared Degradation

| Dataset                 | R-squared | RMSE   |
|-------------------------|----------|--------|
| Training (S1W01--S2W20) | 0.94     | 0.8pp  |
| W11 EU validation       | 0.71     | 3.1pp  |
| W11 NA holdout          | 0.86     | 1.4pp  |

The EU-specific degradation suggests **region-specific confounders** not captured
in the model.

### 4.2 Identified Failure Modes

1. **Concurrent sentiment shock:** The price increase coincided with the Vortex Rifle
   balance controversy and hotfix delay (SAI = 82). The model treats price as an
   independent variable but W11 created a compounding effect.

2. **Cohort shift:** Season 3 brought a large influx of new players (< 90 days tenure).
   The training data overrepresented long-tenure players from Seasons 1--2.

3. **EU regulatory context:** Media coverage of the Belgian notice (EVID-0002) amplified
   player perception of "exploitative pricing," creating a narrative multiplier not
   present in training-period data.

4. **Refund cascade:** Apple/Google one-tap refund flows created a low-friction exit
   path. Training data predates Apple's 2025 refund UI update which reduced refund
   friction by an estimated 60%.

### 4.3 Residual Plot Analysis

Residuals show clear heteroscedasticity: variance increases with price delta magnitude
and decreases with player tenure. This indicates the linear model assumption of
constant variance is violated, particularly for high-delta, low-tenure segments.

## 5. Recommendations

1. **Freeze all pricing experiments** in EU and NA until model is retrained (target:
   W16 at earliest).
2. **Retrain with W11--W13 data** included; add interaction terms for concurrent SAI
   level and cohort-age distribution.
3. **Commission external review** -- engage Analysys Mason or similar econometrics firm
   to audit model specification.
4. **Run controlled A/B test in LATAM** (lowest absolute revenue risk) with 5% price
   delta to validate retrained model before applying to high-revenue regions.
5. **Implement model-confidence circuit breaker:** if predicted vs. actual diverges
   by >2x within first 72 hours, auto-rollback the price change.
6. **Add refund rate as a leading indicator** -- refund spikes preceded churn by 3--5
   days in W11 data and could serve as an early-warning signal.
