## 1. Summary

On 2026-03-07, AetherForge Interactive received a formal notice from the Belgian
Gaming Commission (Kansspelcommissie / Commission des jeux de hasard) asserting
that Eclipse Dominion's "Rift Crate" loot-box mechanic constitutes a game of
chance under Belgian law (Act of 7 May 1999, Art. 2). The notice requires
submission of a compliance remediation plan within 60 calendar days (deadline:
2026-05-06). This document summarizes the notice, broader EU regulatory signals,
and GDPR implications.

## 2. Belgian Gaming Commission Notice

### 2.1 Key Assertions

| Clause | Commission Position                                               |
|--------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| A.1    | "Rift Crates" meet the three-prong test: consideration (real money), chance (randomized outcome), prize (items with tradeable value). |
| A.2    | The "pity timer" (guaranteed rare at 50 opens) does not eliminate the chance element; it merely bounds it. |
| A.3    | The in-game trading marketplace enables secondary-market monetization, satisfying the "prize of value" requirement. |
| B.1    | AetherForge has not obtained a Class E license for games of chance offered via electronic means. |
| B.2    | Continued operation without license or mechanic removal constitutes an ongoing violation subject to daily penalties of up to EUR 25,000. |

### 2.2 Required Response

- **Deadline:** 2026-05-06 (60 days from notice date).
- **Options:** (a) Apply for Class E license, (b) remove or restructure the loot-box
  mechanic in Belgium, or (c) provide legal argumentation that the mechanic does not
  constitute a game of chance.
- **Penalties on non-compliance:** Up to EUR 25,000/day; potential app-store removal
  request to Apple and Google for Belgian storefronts.

## 3. Broader EU Regulatory Signals

### 3.1 Netherlands -- Kansspelautoriteit (KSA)

- **2026-02-28:** Draft guidance document leaked via NOS (Dutch public broadcaster)
  indicating KSA intends to classify paid random-reward mechanics as licensable
  gambling by Q3 2026.
- **Status:** Draft; public consultation expected April 2026.
- **Relevance:** Netherlands is Eclipse Dominion's 3rd-largest EU market by revenue.

### 3.2 European Parliament -- Consumer Protection Committee

- **2026-01-15:** Resolution 2026/2012(INI) adopted, calling on the Commission to
  evaluate loot boxes under the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC).
- **Status:** Non-binding but signals political will.
- **Timeline:** Commission staff working document expected H2 2026.

### 3.3 Germany -- USK/BPjM

- **No active enforcement** against Eclipse Dominion.
- **Risk:** Low-to-moderate. Germany's approach has historically focused on age-rating
  rather than gambling classification. However, the NetzDG amendment pathway could
  introduce disclosure requirements.

## 4. GDPR Implications (Art. 22)

A competitor (unnamed, but believed to be NovaStar Games) was subject to a GDPR
Art. 22 complaint filed with the Irish DPC on 2026-02-10. The complaint asserts
that algorithmic personalization of loot-box offers constitutes "automated individual
decision-making" with legal/significant effects.

### 4.1 Applicability to Eclipse Dominion

| Factor                              | AetherForge Status              |
|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Personalized offer pricing           | Yes -- ML model "Luminary-V3"   |
| Player spending-propensity scoring   | Yes -- internal score 0--100    |
| Automated offer triggers             | Yes -- event-driven via Kafka   |
| Human-in-the-loop override           | Partial -- only for >$50 offers |

### 4.2 Recommended Mitigations

1. Extend human-in-the-loop review to all personalized offers (not just >$50).
2. Implement Art. 22(3) explicit-consent flow for EU players.
3. Prepare Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for Luminary-V3 model.
4. Engage outside counsel (Morrison & Foerster, Brussels office) for Irish DPC
   monitoring.
