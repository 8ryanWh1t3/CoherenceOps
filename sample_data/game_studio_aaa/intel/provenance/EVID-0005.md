## 1. Summary

AetherForge Interactive holds two major esports sponsorship contracts for Eclipse
Dominion: **OrionTel** ($18M/year) and **VantaFuel** ($18M/year). Both contracts
contain performance-linked penalty clauses tied to player sentiment and platform
stability metrics. The W10 Apex Invitational SAI spike (peak = 82) triggered a
near-miss on OrionTel clause 7.2 and a potential breach of VantaFuel clause 4.1.
This document analyzes the relevant clauses and exposure.

## 3. OrionTel -- Clause 7.2 Analysis

### 3.1 Full Clause Text (Summarized)

> **Clause 7.2 -- Sentiment Performance Standard:**
> If the Sentiment Anomaly Index (SAI), as measured by AetherForge's published
> methodology, exceeds seventy-five (75) for more than forty-eight (48)
> consecutive hours during any Sponsored Broadcast Event (as defined in
> Schedule B), Sponsor may elect to (a) reduce the quarterly payment by fifteen
> percent (15%) for the quarter in which the breach occurred, or (b) issue a
> formal cure notice requiring remediation plan within fourteen (14) days.

### 3.2 W10 Apex Invitational Assessment

| Metric                              | Threshold     | Actual          | Status    |
|-------------------------------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| SAI peak                            | > 75          | 82              | Exceeded  |
| Duration above 75                   | > 48h consec. | 6 hours         | Within    |
| Sponsored Broadcast Event?          | Yes (Sched B) | Yes (confirmed) | Applies   |
| **Breach determination**            | --            | --              | **No breach (duration not met)** |

### 3.3 Risk Assessment

- **Near-miss margin:** SAI was above 75 for only 6 hours vs. the 48-hour threshold.
  However, the 6-hour window occurred during peak broadcast viewership (est. 1.4M
  concurrent Twitch viewers).
- **Reputational risk:** OrionTel CMO informally expressed concern in post-event
  debrief call (2026-03-04). No formal notice issued.
- **Future risk:** If W11-level sentiment (SAI avg 60, peak 82) coincides with a
  48+ hour broadcast window (e.g., planned W16 "Dominion World Cup" is a 72-hour
  event), breach is highly probable.
- **Penalty exposure per breach:** 15% of quarterly payment = **$675,000**.

## 4. VantaFuel -- Clause 4.1 Analysis

### 4.1 Full Clause Text (Summarized)

> **Clause 4.1 -- Platform Integrity Standard:**
> Sponsor's obligation to remit monthly payment is contingent upon the Game
> maintaining a Platform Integrity Score (PIS) at or above the Minimum
> Threshold. PIS is defined as a composite of: (a) SAI not exceeding eighty
> (80) at any point during the calendar month, (b) server uptime >= 99.5%
> during Sponsored Activation Hours, and (c) no unresolved cheating scandals
> involving Top 100 ranked players. Failure of any single component
> constitutes a PIS failure and entitles Sponsor to withhold fifty percent
> (50%) of the monthly payment until cure is demonstrated.

### 4.2 W10 Assessment

| Component                  | Threshold       | Actual              | Status     |
|----------------------------|-----------------|---------------------|------------|
| (a) SAI peak < 80          | < 80            | 82                  | **Failed** |
| (b) Uptime >= 99.5%        | >= 99.5%        | 99.2% (matchmaker)  | **Failed** |
| (c) No cheating scandal     | None unresolved | None                | Passed     |
| **PIS determination**      | --              | --                  | **Failed (2 of 3)** |

### 4.3 Financial Exposure

- **Monthly payment:** $1.5M.
- **Withholding on PIS failure:** 50% = **$750,000** for March 2026.
- **Cure mechanism:** AetherForge must demonstrate remediation within 30 days. If
  cured, withheld amount is released. If not cured, withheld amount is forfeited.
- **Current status:** VantaFuel has not yet issued a formal withholding notice.
  Their legal team requested SAI methodology documentation on 2026-03-10, which
  suggests they are evaluating.

## 6. Clause Comparison: Key Differences

| Dimension           | OrionTel 7.2                  | VantaFuel 4.1                   |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| SAI measurement     | Duration-based (48h consec.)  | Instantaneous peak (any moment) |
| Threshold           | SAI > 75                      | SAI > 80                        |
| Penalty type        | Payment reduction (15%)       | Payment withholding (50%)       |
| Cure period         | 14 days                       | 30 days                         |
| Additional triggers | SAI only                      | SAI + uptime + cheating         |

**Critical observation:** VantaFuel's instantaneous-peak trigger is significantly
more aggressive than OrionTel's duration-based trigger. Any future SAI spike above
80, even momentary, triggers the clause.

## 7. Recommendations

1. **Immediate:** Prepare cure documentation for VantaFuel showing W10 remediation
   steps (autoscaler upgrade, SAI monitoring). Target: prevent $750K withholding.
2. **Pre-W16 World Cup:** Implement SAI suppression protocol -- dedicated community
   management, pre-staged hotfix team, 3.5x infrastructure pre-provision.
3. **Contract renegotiation:** At next renewal window, propose:
   - OrionTel: Maintain duration-based trigger but raise threshold to SAI > 80.
   - VantaFuel: Convert instantaneous peak to 4-hour rolling average, aligning
     with OrionTel structure.
4. **Internal tooling:** Deploy real-time SAI dashboard with push alerts at SAI 50
   (warning), 65 (escalation), 75 (crisis) to partnerships and liveops teams.
5. **Legal:** Engage outside counsel to review whether W10 VantaFuel PIS failure
   meets the "force majeure" carve-out in clause 9.3 (unprecedented CCU spike
   argument).
