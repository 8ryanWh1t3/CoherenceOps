## 1. Summary

During the W10 "Apex Invitational" tournament broadcast (2026-03-01 14:00--20:00 UTC),
Eclipse Dominion experienced a concurrent-user (CCU) spike of **2.8x the normal Saturday
peak**. This exceeded the standing assumption of 2.0x (ASM-0005) by 40% and caused
partial service degradation in matchmaking and voice-chat subsystems.

### 3.3 Autoscaler Behavior

- **Trigger threshold:** 70% ALB connection utilization.
- **Scale-out initiated:** 16:31 UTC (14 minutes before peak).
- **Scale-out completed:** 16:40 UTC (9-minute lag).
- **Gap window:** 16:31--16:40 UTC -- during this window, matchmaker queue depth
  exceeded 50,000 sessions, causing queue times >180s for approximately 22% of
  players attempting to join matches.

### 3.4 Downstream Impact

- **Matchmaking:** Median queue time rose from 12s to 47s globally; P95 hit 204s.
- **Voice chat:** Vivox relay saturation caused packet loss >5% for 8 minutes in EU.
- **CDN:** Asset pre-cache hit rate dropped from 97.2% to 88.6% in APAC (new players
  downloading tournament UI assets on first load).

## 4. Methodology Notes

- CCU is defined as unique authenticated sessions with at least one server heartbeat
  in the trailing 60-second window.
- "Normal Saturday peak" baseline (412K) is the median of the four Saturdays in W06--W09,
  excluding any tournament or marketing-event days.
- Latency measurements use the client-embedded heartbeat probe (UDP round-trip / 2),
  sampled at 10% of sessions and extrapolated.

## 5. Recommendations (fed into DLR-20260302-002)

1. Raise autoscaler ceiling from 2.0x to **3.5x** baseline and reduce trigger threshold
   to 55% connection utilization.
2. Pre-provision dedicated tournament capacity **2 hours** before scheduled broadcast.
3. Coordinate with Vivox on dedicated relay allocation for tournament weekends.
4. Implement APAC CDN pre-warm for tournament UI assets 24h before broadcast.
