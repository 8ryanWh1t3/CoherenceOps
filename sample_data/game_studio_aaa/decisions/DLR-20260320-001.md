# DLR-20260320-001: Lore Consistency Framework

## Context

During the Season 3 Rift Collapse live event, JP localization diverged from EN canon on three critical plot points: the Void Sovereign's motivation, the timeline of the Rift opening, and the fate of Agent Kael. DRIFT-20260320-001 captured this as a medium-severity narrative drift after JP community forums identified contradictions that went viral on X/Twitter (4.2M impressions, #EclipsePlotHole trending in JP for 18h). Root cause: JP localization team operated from a stale narrative bible (v1.1, dated 2026-01-15) while EN canon had advanced to v1.3 after emergency MTX-related lore adjustments. No formal process existed for propagating canon updates to localization teams. The CANON-v1.3 update resolved the immediate contradictions, but without a framework, recurrence is certain as Season 4 introduces 14 new narrative beats across 6 languages.

## Options Considered

1. **Canonical Source of Truth with automated sync (selected)** -- single Notion-based narrative bible with per-language branches, automated diff notifications to localization leads on any canon change, and mandatory sign-off gate before localized content ships.
2. **Monthly narrative sync meetings** -- schedule monthly cross-studio narrative alignment calls. Low cost but reactive; does not prevent drift between meetings.
3. **Embedded narrative liaison per studio** -- hire a narrative specialist in each localization studio. Best coverage but +$480K/yr headcount across Tokyo, Berlin, Warsaw.
4. **Post-ship lore audit** -- audit localized content after release and patch discrepancies. Cheapest but damage is done before correction.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Establish a Canonical Source of Truth (CSoT) in Notion with language-specific branches that auto-sync from the EN master. Any change to EN canon triggers a diff notification to all localization leads within 1 hour. No localized narrative content can ship without a signed-off reconciliation against the current canon version. Montreal Narrative Design owns the EN master; each localization studio owns their language branch.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Eliminates stale-bible class of localization drift entirely.
- (+) Automated diff notification reduces human coordination overhead.
- (+) Establishes clear ownership chain: Montreal master, studio branches.
- (-) Notion-based workflow requires all narrative staff to adopt new tooling (est. 2-week onboarding).
- (-) Sign-off gate adds ~4h to localization pipeline per content drop.
- (-) Does not cover non-narrative content (UI strings, item descriptions) -- separate process needed.

## Assumptions

- **ASM-0014**: JP localization resync achievable in 1 sprint (2 weeks).
- **ASM-0006**: Season 4 narrative arc has no lore conflicts (validated against CSoT).

## Kill Switch / Rollback

Revert to manual narrative bible distribution via email. Notion branches remain read-only as reference. Sign-off gate bypassed via `--narrative-override` flag in content pipeline (requires Narrative Director approval). Rollback time: immediate (flag toggle).
