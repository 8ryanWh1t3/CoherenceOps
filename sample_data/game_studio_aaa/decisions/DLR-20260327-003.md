## Context

The Competitive Fairness Protest Response (DLR-20260306-004) addressed the immediate W10 fairness complaints, but the underlying esports rulebook (v2, dated 2025-11-01) predates Eclipse Dominion's competitive ranked mode overhaul and does not account for several scenarios that surfaced during W10-W12: mid-tournament balance patches, server-side latency compensation disputes, cross-region matchmaking fairness, and the protest/pause mechanism that players invoked 14 times during the March tournament. CLAIM-0009 projects that an updated rulebook can reduce competitive protests by 50%. The current rulebook has 23 pages; community feedback (Reddit AMA, 3,200 comments) identified 8 specific gaps. OrionTel and VantaFuel sponsor contracts reference "published competitive rules" as a condition of tournament sponsorship; an outdated rulebook creates contractual ambiguity.

## Options Considered

1. **Comprehensive rulebook v3 with community input (selected)** -- full rewrite addressing all 8 identified gaps, informed by community feedback, pro player advisory input, and W10-W12 incident data. Published publicly with 14-day comment period before ratification.
2. **Addendum to v2** -- patch the 8 gaps as appendix to existing rulebook. Faster but creates fragmented document; gaps between base rules and addendum.
3. **Dynamic rulebook (living document)** -- continuously updated Google Doc. Maximum flexibility but no stable reference point; versioning chaos.
4. **Defer to Season 5** -- wait for next season to overhaul rules. Lowest effort but leaves 8 gaps open for Season 4 remainder.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Publish Esports Rulebook v3 with the following changes:

1. **Mid-tournament balance patch policy**: No balance patches during active tournament brackets. Emergency patches require Tournament Director + Competitive Integrity Lead dual approval (aligns with DLR-20260302-003).
2. **Latency compensation transparency**: Publish server-side tick rate and latency compensation parameters for all tournament servers. Maximum acceptable latency: 80ms (was undocumented).
3. **Cross-region matchmaking rules**: Tournament matches use dedicated regional servers; no cross-region routing. Server selection published 24h before match.
4. **Protest/pause mechanism**: Formal protest procedure with 5-minute pause window. Maximum 2 protests per team per match. Adjudication within 15 minutes by Tournament Official.
5. **Competitive integrity reporting**: Players can report suspected unfair advantages via in-client tool. Response SLA: 24h during tournaments, 72h outside.
6. **Prize pool escrow**: Prize pool funds held in escrow by independent third party; payout within 14 days of tournament conclusion.
7. **Streamer/content creator rules**: Tournament footage rights, delay requirements (90s), and sponsorship disclosure obligations.
8. **Rulebook amendment process**: Changes require 14-day public comment period + Esports Committee vote. Emergency amendments require dual approval (as above).

14-day public comment period opens 2026-03-28; ratified version effective 2026-04-11.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Addresses all 8 community-identified gaps comprehensively.
- (+) Public comment period builds trust and community ownership.
- (+) Stable versioned document satisfies sponsor contractual requirements.
- (+) Protest mechanism reduces ad-hoc disruptions during tournaments.
- (-) 14-day comment period delays effectiveness; one more tournament under v2 rules.
- (-) Community comments may surface additional demands beyond scope.
- (-) Published latency parameters may invite technical gaming/exploitation.
