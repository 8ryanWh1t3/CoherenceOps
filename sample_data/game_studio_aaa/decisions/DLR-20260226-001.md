## Context

Eclipse Dominion Season 3 used standalone weekly episodes with no overarching narrative. Player engagement with lore content dropped 18% over the season. Competitor titles with serialized arcs show 2.3x higher lore-content completion rates. Season 4 must establish a compelling three-act narrative arc to drive engagement and justify the canon-level investment in 14-language localization across 12 weekly episodes.

## Options Considered

1. **Three-act serialized arc across 12 episodes (selected)** -- "The Dominion Fracture" storyline with persistent consequences. Canon-level commitment.
2. **Anthology format** -- self-contained weekly stories with shared setting. Lower risk, lower engagement ceiling.
3. **Player-choice branching narrative** -- community votes determine plot direction. High engagement but unpredictable production costs and localization nightmare.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Commit to "The Dominion Fracture" three-act arc: Act I (episodes 1-4) establishes the faction split, Act II (5-9) escalates the conflict, Act III (10-12) resolves with permanent map and lore changes. Full localization in 14 languages. Berlin studio leads localization QA.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Serialized narrative drives weekly return engagement (+15-20% projected lore completion).
- (+) Canon-level permanence creates long-term world-building value.
- (-) Locked into 12-episode commitment; mid-season pivot is expensive ($1.4M sunk cost by episode 4).
- (-) Localization in 14 languages at canon-level quality costs 40% more than standalone episodes.

## Assumptions

- **ASM-0012**: Voice actor contracts for 6 principal cast members are secured through Q2 2026.

If lore completion drops below 25% by episode 4, defer remaining episodes to standalone format. Pre-produced standalone episode scripts (3 backup) available for immediate swap. Sunk cost at episode 4 abort: ~$1.4M.
