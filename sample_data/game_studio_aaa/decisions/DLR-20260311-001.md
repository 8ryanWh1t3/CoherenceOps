## Context

Following the EU MTX rollback (DLR-20260309-001), AetherForge's EU legal counsel has advised that the Belgian Gaming Commission (BGC) is preparing formal guidance classifying Eclipse Dominion's randomized loot boxes as gambling under Belgian law. Similar regulatory signals are emerging from the Netherlands and Germany. AetherForge's current EU storefront includes 23 randomized loot box SKUs across cosmetic and battle pass tiers. Maintaining these SKUs exposes the company to enforcement action, fines up to 1.6M EUR per violation under Belgian law, and potential EU-wide regulatory contagion.

The decision to convert loot boxes to direct-purchase bundles must be executed before the BGC publishes formal guidance (estimated: late March 2026).

## Options Considered

1. **Convert all EU loot boxes to direct-purchase cosmetic bundles (selected)** -- replace all 23 randomized SKUs with curated bundles at fixed prices. Players see exactly what they purchase.
2. **Remove loot boxes, no replacement** -- eliminate the SKUs entirely. Simplest compliance path but sacrifices all associated revenue.
3. **Implement pity timer / probability disclosure** -- keep loot boxes but add guaranteed-item-at-N-pulls and publish drop rates. May satisfy some regulators but unlikely to satisfy Belgium.
4. **Geo-fence Belgium only** -- restrict loot boxes to Belgian IPs only. Narrowest compliance scope but risks whack-a-mole as other EU states follow.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Convert all 23 randomized loot box SKUs in the EU storefront to direct-purchase cosmetic bundles. Bundle pricing based on median expected value of the replaced loot box (rounded to nearest $0.99 increment). A/B testing coverage is waived for initial deployment to meet the BGC timeline -- full A/B validation of bundle pricing and conversion rates must be completed by WAIVER-EXPIRY (2026-04-11). Deployment target: 2026-03-18 for all EU regions.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Eliminates regulatory exposure in Belgium, Netherlands, and Germany.
- (+) Positions AetherForge as industry leader in player-friendly monetization.
- (+) Direct-purchase model has higher per-unit margin (no randomization overhead).
- (-) Annual EU MTX revenue drops by an estimated $6.2M (loot box revenue).
- (-) Partially offset by +$2.1M direct purchase uplift (net: -$4.1M).
- (-) Waiver of A/B testing increases risk of suboptimal bundle pricing at launch.
- (-) Players who preferred loot box gambling mechanics may churn.

## Kill Switch / Rollback

Disable EU storefront entirely (nuclear option). This removes all purchase capability for EU players. Authorization required: CEO + General Counsel. Estimated revenue impact of full storefront suspension: -$8.4M/quarter. This is the option of last resort and should only be triggered if direct-purchase bundles themselves face regulatory challenge.
