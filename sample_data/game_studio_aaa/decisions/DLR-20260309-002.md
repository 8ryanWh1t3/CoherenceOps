## Context

During Season 4 content audit, Berlin localization team discovered that the Japanese (JP) language pack for Eclipse Dominion has diverged from the English-canonical (EN) source on character backstory for three playable characters: Kael Vortex, Zara Nine, and Commander Thresh. The divergence originated in the Season 3 mid-season update when JP translators received an outdated narrative brief. Affected text spans 42 dialogue entries, 8 lore codex pages, and 3 character biography screens. The divergence was flagged by Tokyo studio QA via DRIFT-20260320-001 after JP community members noted inconsistencies between JP and EN wikis. All 14 language packs require audit to determine if similar drift occurred elsewhere.

## Options Considered

1. **72-hour full localization sync sprint (selected)** -- audit all 14 language packs against EN-canonical source. Fix JP immediately, flag any other divergences for same-sprint correction.
2. **JP-only hotfix** -- fix the known JP divergence and defer full audit to next content milestone. Faster but risks undiscovered drift in other languages.
3. **Automated diff with manual review** -- build tooling first, then audit. Better long-term but adds 2-week delay to JP fix.
4. **Ship corrections in Season 4 mid-season patch** -- bundle with scheduled content update. Lowest disruption but JP community exposure continues for 3+ weeks.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Execute a 72-hour sprint across Berlin and Warsaw localization teams to audit all 14 language packs against the EN-canonical source (CANON-v1.2 tag). JP corrections are highest priority and must ship within 48 hours as a standalone localization hotfix. All other language corrections ship within 72 hours. Berlin leads the audit; Warsaw provides QA gating for all corrections.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Comprehensive audit catches all localization drift, not just JP.
- (+) JP fix ships quickly, limiting community exposure.
- (+) Establishes a reusable audit process for future content drops.
- (-) $180K overtime cost for the 72-hour sprint across two studios.
- (-) Berlin and Warsaw teams are diverted from Season 4 mid-season localization work (3-day slip).
- (-) Sprint pace increases risk of introducing new translation errors.

## Assumptions
