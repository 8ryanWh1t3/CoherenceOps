# DLR-20260323-001: Studio Handoff Protocol for Live Patches

## Context

Cross-timezone handoff gaps between LA (UTC-8), Montreal (UTC-5), and Tokyo (UTC+9) caused measurable delays during the W10-W12 crisis period. When LA engineering completed a hotfix at 18:00 PST, no Montreal or Tokyo team was available to pick up validation until their next business day -- a 10-14h dead zone. During the Emergency Hotfix Bypass Protocol (DLR-20260306-002) and Cross-Studio Hotfix Cadence Alignment (DLR-20260313-003), three separate hotfixes sat idle in the handoff gap, adding a cumulative 38h of unnecessary delay. For sev-1 incidents, this gap is unacceptable: every hour of downtime during peak hours costs an estimated $42K in lost revenue and $8K in sponsor SLA exposure.

## Options Considered

1. **Formal handoff protocol with overlap shifts (selected)** -- define 2h overlap windows between studio pairs (LA-Montreal, Montreal-Tokyo, Tokyo-LA). Handoff checklist in PagerDuty with mandatory acknowledgment. On-call engineer in each studio during overlap.
2. **Follow-the-sun single on-call** -- one global on-call rotation. Simplest but requires engineers to work outside business hours regularly; burnout risk.
3. **LA-only 24/7 ops center** -- staff a 24/7 operations center in LA. Best response time but $1.2M/yr staffing cost and single point of failure.
4. **Async handoff via Slack + Jira** -- improve existing Slack-based handoff with Jira ticket automation. Low cost but no guaranteed acknowledgment; gaps persist.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Establish formal handoff protocol with three defined overlap windows: LA-Montreal (13:00-15:00 EST / 10:00-12:00 PST), Montreal-Tokyo (21:00-23:00 JST / 07:00-09:00 EST), Tokyo-LA (08:00-10:00 PST / 01:00-03:00 JST+1). Each overlap window has a designated handoff engineer per studio. PagerDuty integration requires acknowledgment within 15 minutes or auto-escalation. Handoff checklist covers: patch status, test results, blockers, next actions, and escalation contacts.

## Trade-offs

- (+) Eliminates 10-14h dead zones between studios.
- (+) Structured checklist ensures no context lost during handoff.
- (+) PagerDuty integration provides audit trail for compliance.
- (-) Overlap shifts require schedule adjustments for ~12 engineers across 3 studios.
- (-) Tokyo 01:00-03:00 JST window is unsociable; rotation needed to avoid burnout.
- (-) Monthly cost of overlap shift premiums: ~$18K.

## Assumptions

- **DLR-20260306-002**: Emergency hotfix bypass protocol remains in effect for sev-1.
- **DLR-20260313-003**: Cross-studio hotfix cadence alignment defines the patch types that flow through handoff.
