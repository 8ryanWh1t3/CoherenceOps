# DLR-20260313-001: Patch Cadence SLA Revision

|---------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Status**    | Accepted                                   |
| **Date**      | 2026-03-13                                 |
| **Owner**     | Release Engineering (LA)                   |
| **Studios**   | LA, Montreal, Tokyo, Warsaw                |
| **Domain**    | Patch Cadence / SLA                        |

## Context

During Week 11, a severity-1 matchmaking bug took 96 hours to patch -- double the 48-hour internal SLA target established in DLR-20260228-001. Root cause analysis revealed that the existing SLA tiers were unrealistic given the multi-studio review and QA requirements. The 48-hour target for all severity levels created a false equivalence between critical live-service issues and lower-priority bugs, leading to triage paralysis. DRIFT-20260313-001 flagged the SLA breach as a recurring pattern (3 of last 5 sev-1 issues exceeded target).

## Options Considered

1. **Tiered SLA revision with escalation matrix (selected)** -- differentiate targets by severity: sev-1 = 24h, high = 72h, medium = 1 week. Add a formal escalation matrix with named owners at each tier.
2. **Uniform reduction to 36h** -- tighten the single SLA target. Simpler but still conflates severity levels.
3. **SLA per studio** -- each studio owns its own SLA targets. Provides autonomy but complicates cross-studio coordination.
4. **Eliminate SLA, use priority queue** -- abandon fixed-time targets in favor of a continuously prioritized queue. More agile but harder to hold teams accountable.

## Decision

Adopt Option 1. Revise the patch cadence SLA to three tiers:

| Severity | Response Time | Patch Deployed | Escalation at |
|----------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Sev-1 (critical) | 30 min | 24 hours | 12 hours (VP Engineering) |
| High     | 2 hours       | 72 hours       | 48 hours (Director)       |
| Medium   | 8 hours       | 1 week         | 5 days (Manager)          |

Escalation matrix: if a patch exceeds 50% of its target time without a clear resolution path, the named escalation owner is automatically paged and must either authorize additional resources or formally extend the SLA with justification logged in the incident tracker.

Suspend SLA targets during declared emergencies (defined as: CEO or CTO declares a company-wide emergency affecting >25% of DAU).

## Trade-offs

- (+) Sev-1 target reduced from 48h to 24h -- aligned with player expectations.
- (+) Tiered system prevents triage paralysis by clearly differentiating urgency.
- (+) Escalation matrix provides accountability without micromanagement.
- (+) Emergency suspension clause prevents SLA gaming during genuine crises.
- (-) 24h sev-1 target is aggressive and may require the bypass protocol (DLR-20260306-002).
- (-) Escalation burden on VP Engineering for sev-1 issues.
- (-) Medium-tier SLA (1 week) may feel too lenient to community.

## Assumptions

- **ASM-0003**: Existing QA regression suite catches >95% of gameplay-affecting bugs before production. The 24h sev-1 target assumes that targeted smoke tests (not full regression) are sufficient for sev-1 hotfixes, consistent with the bypass protocol.

## Kill Switch / Rollback

Suspend all SLA targets during declared emergencies. Outside of emergencies, revert to the flat 48-hour SLA (DLR-20260228-001 baseline) if tiered system creates coordination overhead that exceeds benefit. Activation: Release Engineering lead + any Director.
