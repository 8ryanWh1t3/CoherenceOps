## Narrative Arc

### W09 — Baseline (Feb 24-28)

Steady-state operations. Eclipse Dominion Season 7 running at expected capacity. All KPIs within normal operating bands. 1.2M DAU, $9.2M weekly revenue. Infrastructure healthy. Coherence score 88/100 — the highest it would reach in this arc. The calm before the storm.

### W10 — Tournament Spike (Mar 3-7)

Eclipse Dominion World Championship qualifiers drove a 50% DAU surge (1.8M) and a 174% CCU spike (1.15M). Revenue peaked at $14.1M, with esports bundles contributing $3.67M (2.66x baseline). The event was a commercial success but exposed critical infrastructure gaps:

- **APAC infrastructure buckled.** Tokyo relay latency hit 112ms p50 (2.5x baseline). ASM-0005 ("APAC infra headroom sufficient for 2x load") expired when actual load reached 2.8x.
- **Build pipeline saturated.** P95 build times nearly doubled to 34.2 minutes.
- **Support tickets tripled** to 9,600, dominated by APAC matchmaking complaints.
- **Pricing inconsistency seeded.** Esports bundles launched with regional pricing that would prove catastrophic in W11.

Coherence dropped from 88 to 71. Three drift signals emerged. Emergency APAC capacity expansion authorized (DLR-0031).

### W11 — Monetization Backlash (Mar 10-14)

The crisis week. Players discovered that EU cosmetic bundles were priced 40% above NA equivalents — a byproduct of the rushed esports bundle rollout in W10. Social media amplification turned a pricing error into a brand crisis.

**Impact:**
- EU DAU cratered 18% below baseline (285K vs 348K).
- EU ARPPU collapsed from $14.80 to $8.90 as spending whales boycotted.
- EU churn spiked to 8.2% (4.8x baseline).
- Global refund rate surged to 6.8% (17x baseline).
- Support tickets peaked at 14,200 (4.4x baseline).
- German consumer protection authority (BfJ) issued an informal inquiry.

Two assumptions expired: ASM-0007 (EU pricing parity) and ASM-0009 (refund rate ceiling). Five drift signals active. Coherence score hit its nadir at 52/100. Assumption health bottomed out at 41.

### W12 — Recovery Begins (Mar 17-21)

Decisive action reversed the decline. Key interventions:

1. **Kill switch invoked** (DLR-0034): All EU cosmetic bundles rolled back to NA-parity pricing within 2 hours.
2. **Goodwill credit program** (DLR-0035): Affected EU players received store credit equal to the pricing disparity.
3. **JP localization hotfix** deployed, resolving currency symbol errors (DRIFT-0017 closed).
4. **APAC capacity expansion** (DLR-0031) completed — Tokyo latency normalized.
5. **Sponsor communication protocol** (DLR-0036) ratified to address brand safety concerns from NovaTech and Pulse Energy.

EU DAU stabilized at 299K (no longer falling). Churn slowed. Refund rate halved. SAI dropped to 58 as infrastructure healed. Coherence recovered to 64.

### W13 — Stabilization (Mar 24-27)

Trajectory turned positive across most metrics. DAU recovered to 96% of baseline. Revenue gap narrowed to -3% vs baseline. Key developments:

- **Cosmetic rebalance** (DLR-0037): 120 items repriced with transparent regional parity. Community reception cautiously positive.
- **EU D7 retention** recovering: 38.0% (up from 28.1% nadir, target 42%).
- **LATAM outperforming**: DAU and ARPPU both slightly above baseline, driven by Brazilian tournament viewership halo.
- **Infrastructure stable**: SAI at 45, approaching baseline of 34.
- **Churn model broken** (DRIFT-0019): W11 event invalidated predictive model. Rebuild authorized (DLR-0038).

Coherence recovered to 76/100. Three drift signals remain active (regulatory, sponsor, churn model).
