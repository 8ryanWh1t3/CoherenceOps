# DRIFT-20260320-001: Narrative Localization Divergence JP vs EN

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Severity** | medium |
| **Status** | patched |
| **Detected** | 2026-03-20 |
| **Reporter** | Localization Lead (Tokyo) |
| **Studio** | Tokyo, Montreal |
| **Region** | APAC (JP) |

## Signal

On 2026-03-20, Tokyo's Localization Lead identified a critical narrative divergence between the Japanese and English versions of Eclipse Dominion's Season 3 finale questline, "Rift Collapse." The JP localization omitted a key plot beat -- the reveal that Commander Voss orchestrated the Rift Collapse as a deliberate sabotage -- reducing a 4-scene narrative arc to 3 scenes. The omitted scene (Scene 3 of 4: "Voss Confession") contained 847 words of dialogue and 3 cinematic triggers.

The divergence was introduced during the W10-W11 emergency patch cycle. The localization pipeline was bypassed for patches 4.0.7 through 4.1.1 (per emergency protocol invoked under DLR-20260309-002) to accelerate deployment. The EN client received the full Rift Collapse narrative in patch 4.0.8 (2026-03-07). The JP client received a partial narrative in the same patch because the JP localization of Scene 3 was in progress but not complete when the emergency bypass was invoked. The pipeline bypass flag was not reset after the emergency period ended, so the JP Scene 3 delivery was silently dropped from patches 4.0.9 through 4.1.1.

The divergence was discovered by Japanese lore community members on the Eclipse Dominion JP forums, where a comparison thread accumulated 2,400 replies and 18,000 views within 48 hours. The thread's title translates to "Where is the Voss betrayal? EN players know the truth but JP is left in the dark." The JP lore community's reaction was sharply negative: 340 1-star reviews were posted on the JP PlayStation Store within 24 hours, and JP player NPS dropped 22 points week-over-week (from 41 to 19).

## Impact

- **Player sentiment (JP)**: NPS drop of 22 points in JP market. JP contributes 12% of global DAU (approximately 84,000 daily players). The lore-engaged segment (estimated 18% of JP DAU, ~15,100 players) is disproportionately affected.
- **Store ratings**: 340 1-star JP PlayStation Store reviews. Eclipse Dominion's JP store rating dropped from 4.2 to 3.8 stars. PlayStation's algorithm weights recent reviews heavily; the rating drop reduces storefront visibility and organic discovery.
- **Canon integrity**: The JP client is effectively operating on an incomplete canon. JP players are on CANON-v1.1 narrative content while EN players are on CANON-v1.3. This creates a canon version gap that affects cross-region discourse, wiki content, and community-created lore guides.
- **Revenue**: JP MTX revenue showed a 6.4% decline in the 72 hours post-discovery ($42K revenue impact over 3 days). Extrapolated monthly impact: -$420K if unresolved.

## Root Cause

The root cause is the emergency localization bypass protocol (DLR-20260309-002) and the absence of a "pipeline bypass reset" mechanism:

1. **Emergency bypass**: DLR-20260309-002 authorized bypassing the localization pipeline for patches 4.0.7-4.1.1 to accelerate EU regulatory response. This was appropriate for monetization-related changes (pricing, storefront) but was applied globally, including to narrative content.
2. **No bypass reset**: The bypass flag in the CI/CD pipeline is a boolean (`SKIP_LOC_REVIEW=true`) that was set on 2026-03-09 and never reset. No automated expiry or manual checklist item existed to restore the flag after the emergency period.
3. **Silent drop**: When the JP localization of Scene 3 was completed on 2026-03-12, the delivery job attempted to merge it into the 4.1.0 build but was blocked by the bypass flag. The job logged a warning (`LOC-JP-SKIP: scene3_voss_confession.json not merged, SKIP_LOC_REVIEW=true`) but the warning was routed to a log aggregator with no alert threshold configured for localization skip events.

## Patch / Resolution

Resolved on 2026-03-22 via three actions:

1. **Narrative hotfix** (deployed 2026-03-21, patch 4.1.4): JP Scene 3 ("Voss Confession") delivered to JP client. Full narrative parity with EN confirmed. Tokyo localization team completed a 47-string audit of all Season 3 content to verify no additional omissions.
2. **Pipeline bypass reset protocol**: The `SKIP_LOC_REVIEW` flag now has a mandatory expiry of 72 hours. After expiry, the flag auto-resets to `false` and sends a notification to the Localization Lead in each studio. Manual extension requires VP-level approval with documented justification.
3. **Lore consistency framework** (DLR-20260320-001): Established a cross-region narrative parity gate in the CI/CD pipeline. Every build that includes narrative content must pass a localization completeness check -- all supported languages must have >98% string coverage before the build is promoted to production. Builds below threshold are held with a `LOC-INCOMPLETE` status and escalated to the Localization Lead.

Community response to the fix was positive: the JP forum thread was updated with a developer response (in Japanese) acknowledging the error and explaining the fix. 890 of the 2,400 thread participants responded favorably. JP PlayStation Store rating recovered to 4.0 within 5 days of the hotfix.
