# DRIFT-20260313-001: Patch Cadence SLA Breached: 48h to 96h

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Severity** | high |
| **Status** | patched |
| **Detected** | 2026-03-13 |
| **Reporter** | Release Engineering Lead (LA) |
| **Studio** | Warsaw, Montreal, LA |
| **Region** | Global |

## Signal

On 2026-03-13 at 09:14 UTC, the 48-hour SLA defined in DLR-20260228-001 was breached for the first time since its adoption. A high-severity weapon balance hotfix (addressing a 12% damage variance on the "Void Reaper" assault rifle discovered during tournament replays) took 96 hours from triage classification to deployed hotfix -- exactly 2x the SLA ceiling.

The SLA clock started at 2026-03-09 13:00 UTC when the Warsaw QA triage lead classified the drift signal as high severity. The hotfix was deployed at 2026-03-13 13:22 UTC. The breach affected all regions simultaneously because weapon balance is server-authoritative and globally synchronized.

During the 96-hour exposure window, 2,847 ranked matches were played with the imbalanced weapon active. Competitive integrity complaints spiked: 1,420 support tickets filed citing "unfair weapon advantage" and 340 reports of suspected exploiting (players deliberately using the Void Reaper to gain SR). The esports community reaction was swift -- 4 professional teams issued public statements questioning AetherForge's competitive integrity commitment.

## Impact

- **Competitive integrity**: 2,847 ranked matches affected. Estimated 14,200 players exposed to imbalanced gameplay. 890 SR adjustments required post-patch.
- **Player trust**: Support ticket volume for "balance" category increased 340% over 96 hours. Competitive player NPS dropped 8 points (from 34 to 26).
- **Sponsor exposure**: Both OrionTel and VantaFuel sponsor contracts include a 48-hour response clause for competitive integrity issues (Section 7.2). This breach triggers the renegotiation provision. Combined annual sponsorship value at risk: $30M.
- **SLA precedent**: First breach of DLR-20260228-001. If the SLA is breached within its first 30 days, credibility of the entire patch cadence framework is undermined.

## Root Cause

Two compounding failures created the 48-hour overrun:

1. **Warsaw QA backlog**: Warsaw was simultaneously processing regression tests for the EU MTX rollback (DLR-20260309-001), the loot-box-to-direct-purchase conversion (DLR-20260311-001), and routine Season 4 patch validation. The QA team had capacity for 2 concurrent test tracks but was running 3. The weapon balance hotfix entered the queue behind the MTX conversion tests and waited 34 hours before testing began.
2. **Montreal holiday gap**: March 11-12 was a provincial holiday in Quebec (Journee nationale des patriotes observed). The Montreal balance team -- responsible for authoring the weapon tuning delta -- had a 36-hour coverage gap. The follow-the-sun rotation failed because the backup balance engineer (LA) was unfamiliar with the Void Reaper tuning parameters and required a 6-hour knowledge transfer from the returning Montreal lead.

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: [DLR-20260228-001](../decisions/DLR-20260228-001.md) -- Patch Tuesday SLA definition (the SLA that was breached).
- **Upstream (indirect)**: The Warsaw QA overload was caused by the cascade from [DRIFT-20260309-001](DRIFT-20260309-001.md) and [DLR-20260311-001](../decisions/DLR-20260311-001.md) consuming QA capacity.
- **Downstream**: [DRIFT-20260313-002](DRIFT-20260313-002.md) -- Sponsor renegotiation triggered (direct consequence of SLA breach).
- **Downstream**: [DRIFT-20260319-001](DRIFT-20260319-001.md) -- Warsaw QA pipeline bottleneck (the backlog condition persisted).

## Patch / Resolution

Resolved on 2026-03-15 via three actions:

1. **Hotfix deployed**: The Void Reaper damage variance was corrected in patch 4.1.3 (deployed 2026-03-13 13:22 UTC). 890 SR adjustments applied retroactively to affected ranked matches within 24 hours.
2. **QA capacity expansion**: DLR-20260313-001 authorized emergency QA contractor engagement for Warsaw -- 8 additional QA engineers onboarded by 2026-03-17 for a 6-week term. Cost: $192K.
3. **Holiday coverage protocol**: DLR-20260313-002 established mandatory cross-studio handoff documentation for any balance or hotfix work in progress when a studio enters a holiday window. The "hot handoff" protocol requires a 90-minute recorded knowledge transfer session before any coverage gap exceeding 12 hours.
