## Signal

Warsaw's QA Director flagged a sustained pipeline bottleneck in the QA testing infrastructure during the W12 capacity review. The Warsaw QA studio has been running 3 concurrent test tracks since 2026-03-09, but its validated capacity is 2 concurrent tracks. The overload condition, first identified as a contributing factor in the SLA breach (DRIFT-20260313-001), has persisted for 10 days despite the emergency QA contractor onboarding (DLR-20260313-001).

Current test track allocation: (1) Season 4.1 regression suite -- 847 test cases, 72-hour cycle; (2) EU storefront conversion validation -- 312 test cases, 48-hour cycle; (3) weapon balance patch queue -- rolling intake, averaging 23 hotfix candidates per week. Combined workload requires 168 engineer-hours/week; available capacity (including 8 contractors) is 142 engineer-hours/week -- a 15.4% deficit.

The bottleneck has caused test turnaround to slip from the target 48 hours to a sustained 96 hours across all tracks. This means every patch, hotfix, and storefront change is spending an extra 48 hours in the QA queue before deployment. The downstream effect is a systematic drag on AetherForge's ability to ship fixes and improvements at the cadence required by the 48-hour SLA (DLR-20260228-001).

Three patches are currently blocked in the Warsaw queue: a matchmaking fairness adjustment (Montreal, submitted 2026-03-17), a LATAM localization string update (Berlin, submitted 2026-03-18), and an anti-cheat signature update (Warsaw internal, submitted 2026-03-18). The anti-cheat update is particularly time-sensitive -- a new exploit vector was reported on 2026-03-16 and the signature update is the primary mitigation.

## Impact

- **Patch velocity**: All patches experiencing 48-hour additional delay. Season 4 shipped an average of 2.3 patches/week in W09; current rate is 1.1 patches/week due to QA bottleneck.
- **SLA risk**: The 48-hour SLA (DLR-20260228-001) is structurally at risk for any new sev-1 or high-severity drift signal. If the QA queue is full when a critical hotfix arrives, the SLA breach pattern from DRIFT-20260313-001 will recur.
- **Anti-cheat exposure**: The blocked anti-cheat signature update means the exploit vector remains active. 127 suspected exploit accounts detected since 2026-03-16; estimated 3,400 ranked matches affected.
- **Team health**: Warsaw QA engineers have been working 52-hour weeks (vs 40-hour standard) for 10 consecutive days. Two senior QA leads have flagged burnout concerns. Sustained overload increases defect escape risk.

## Root Cause

The Warsaw bottleneck is a capacity planning failure compounded by crisis response:

1. **Baseline under-provisioning**: Warsaw's validated capacity of 2 concurrent test tracks was adequate for normal operations but left zero headroom for concurrent crisis response. The Season 4 EU regulatory crisis consumed an entire test track that was not planned.
2. **Contractor ramp-up delay**: The 8 emergency contractors (DLR-20260313-001) onboarded on 2026-03-17 but require approximately 2 weeks to reach full productivity on Eclipse Dominion's test infrastructure. Current contractor productivity is estimated at 40% of a full-time Warsaw QA engineer.
3. **Test suite growth**: The Season 4.1 regression suite grew 34% from Season 4.0 (632 to 847 test cases) due to the storefront conversion. No corresponding capacity increase was planned.

## Patch / Resolution

**Status: In Progress.** Three-track resolution:

1. **Immediate triage** (in effect): Warsaw QA Director has implemented priority queuing -- sev-1 and high-severity hotfixes jump the queue and are tested within 24 hours regardless of current track load. Medium and low severity items may experience up to 120-hour turnaround until the bottleneck clears.
2. **Automated regression gate** (DLR-20260320-002, deploying 2026-03-24): Automated smoke test suite covering 340 of the 847 regression test cases. This reduces the manual test burden by approximately 40% and is expected to bring turnaround back to 60 hours (still above 48-hour target but substantially improved).
3. **Permanent capacity expansion** (DLR pending, target 2026-04-01): Request to add 4 permanent QA engineers to Warsaw, increasing validated capacity from 2 to 3 concurrent test tracks. Estimated annual cost: $480K. Business case: the SLA breach (DRIFT-20260313-001) cost an estimated $7.8M in sponsor renegotiation exposure -- the capacity expansion pays for itself 16x over.

Target resolution: Turnaround returns to <48 hours by W15 (2026-04-06) when automated regression gate is fully operational and contractors reach 80% productivity.
