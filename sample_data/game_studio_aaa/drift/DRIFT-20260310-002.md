## Signal

EU Daily Active Users (DAU) dropped from 380,000 (7-day rolling average as of March 8) to 312,000 on March 10, an 18% decline over 72 hours. The decline accelerated through the March 9-10 window following the Belgian regulatory notice (DRIFT-20260309-001) and the MTX refund surge (DRIFT-20260310-001). By March 11, EU DAU had further declined to 304,000 (20% below the March 8 baseline).

The DAU decline was not uniform across EU markets:

| Market | Pre-Crisis DAU | March 10 DAU | Decline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Belgium | 11,800 | 7,200 | -39% |
| Netherlands | 19,400 | 12,600 | -35% |
| Germany | 82,000 | 68,900 | -16% |
| France | 61,000 | 52,500 | -14% |
| UK | 74,000 | 65,100 | -12% |
| Nordics | 38,000 | 33,800 | -11% |
| Other EU | 93,800 | 71,900 | -23% |

Belgium and the Netherlands -- the two markets directly implicated in the regulatory news -- experienced the most severe declines (-39% and -35% respectively). The "Other EU" category's -23% decline includes significant drops in Spain (-26%), Italy (-22%), and Poland (-19%), markets where the regulatory news was amplified by local gaming media.

Session duration also declined: EU average session length dropped from 42 minutes to 31 minutes (-26%), and average sessions per DAU declined from 2.3 to 1.8 (-22%). Combined, total EU play-hours dropped 38% from the March 8 baseline.

The DAU decline correlates with but is not fully explained by the refund cohort. Of the 14,200 players who requested refunds (DRIFT-20260310-001), approximately 8,800 (62%) became inactive. The remaining 59,200 DAU decline (68,000 total decline minus 8,800 refund-churners) represents players who reduced or stopped engagement without requesting refunds -- a "soft churn" pattern indicating broader sentiment damage.

## Impact

- **Financial**: EU contributes $134.4M/yr in MTX revenue (28% of global $480M) and $38.2M/yr in ad revenue. An 18% DAU decline, if sustained for 30 days, would translate to approximately $7.8M in lost EU revenue ($5.2M MTX + $2.6M ad revenue). If the decline compounds through reduced social/viral acquisition, 90-day projected loss: $14.2M.
- **Growth metrics**: EU new player acquisition rate dropped 31% in the March 9-11 window (from 8,400/day to 5,800/day), indicating that the negative press cycle is suppressing organic installs in addition to driving existing player churn.
- **Seasonal risk**: The DAU decline is occurring 3 weeks before the planned Season 5 launch (March 30). Season launches typically drive a 25-35% DAU uplift. If the EU player base enters Season 5 at the depressed 312K level, the seasonal uplift ceiling is significantly reduced: projected Season 5 EU peak of 390K-421K vs. the pre-crisis projection of 475K-513K.
- **Competitive positioning**: EU market share data (Newzoo/Sensor Tower) shows Eclipse Dominion's EU share of the competitive FPS category declining from 18.2% (February) to an estimated 15.1% (March 10), with share shifting to competitors Apex Legends (gaining ~1.4pp) and VALORANT (gaining ~0.9pp).

## Root Cause

The EU DAU decline has three contributing layers:

1. **Regulatory sentiment shock** (primary): The Belgian Gaming Commission notice and Dutch Kansspelautoriteit leak created a "is this game going away?" narrative among EU players. Gaming media headlines like "Eclipse Dominion Faces Gambling Crackdown in Europe" (Eurogamer) and "Belgian Commission Targets Eclipse Dominion Loot Boxes" (GamesIndustry.biz) introduced uncertainty about the game's future in EU markets.

2. **Refund-driven churn** (secondary): The 14,200 refund cohort (DRIFT-20260310-001) included a disproportionate number of high-engagement players (average 6.2 sessions/week vs. EU average 4.1). Their departure removed social anchors from friend groups and guilds, triggering network-effect churn in their communities.

3. **Residual tournament crisis sentiment** (tertiary): The EU player base was still recovering from the tournament service degradation (DRIFT-20260302-001 cascade). EU NPS had recovered to +28 (from the +18 trough on March 5) but had not returned to the pre-crisis +42. The regulatory news hit a player base with already-depleted trust reserves.

The February 15 MTX price increase (15% across EU markets) is identified as a priming factor. While it did not cause churn by itself, it shifted 8-12% of the EU player base into a "dissatisfied but still playing" state that was easily tipped into inactivity by the regulatory news.

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Win-back campaign** (Mar 12): Berlin marketing team launched a targeted re-engagement campaign for the 68,000 lapsed EU players. Offer: 7-day free Battle Pass trial + 500 premium currency (value: EUR 4.99). Campaign delivered via email (42% open rate) and push notification (18% tap rate). Early results (Mar 12-15): 12,400 lapsed players returned (18.2% win-back rate).
2. **EU community events** (Mar 13-20): Berlin community team organized 4 EU-specific in-game events (Benelux Community Cup, DACH Championship, French Open, UK Masters) with dedicated prize pools ($5K each) to re-energize the EU competitive community. Participation: 24,600 unique players across all 4 events.
3. **EU pricing stabilization** (Mar 11, aligned with DRIFT-20260310-001 resolution): The Dominion Crates price rollback removed a friction point for returning players. Communicated proactively in the win-back campaign messaging.
4. **Season 5 EU preview** (Mar 15): Early access to Season 5 trailer and roadmap for EU players (1 week ahead of global reveal). Designed to counter the "is the game going away?" narrative by demonstrating continued investment. The preview generated 2.8M views across EU social channels and 89% positive sentiment.
5. **DAU trajectory as of Mar 20**: EU DAU has partially recovered to 341,000 (still -10.3% vs. pre-crisis 380K baseline). Belgium and Netherlands remain depressed (-28% and -24% respectively). The recovery trend suggests EU DAU may reach 360K by March 30 (Season 5 launch), still 5.3% below baseline. Full recovery to 380K is projected for mid-April, contingent on Season 5 reception and absence of further regulatory escalation.
6. **Status**: In progress. Weekly monitoring via TELEMETRY reports. Next review checkpoint: March 27 (pre-Season 5 readiness assessment).
