## Signal

During Day 3 of the Apex Invitational, APAC server latency breached critical thresholds with P50 latency hitting 94ms (baseline: 55ms) and P99 latency reaching 231ms (baseline: 85ms) across the Tokyo, Singapore, and Sydney Points of Presence. The degradation began at 05:48 UTC on March 4, coinciding with APAC prime-time (14:48 JST / 13:48 SGT / 16:48 AEDT), and persisted for 3 hours 42 minutes until 09:30 UTC.

At peak degradation (07:15 UTC), 186,000 APAC players were on servers with P50 latency above 80ms. Of these, approximately 34,000 players on the Sydney PoP experienced the worst conditions with P50 latency of 142ms and P99 of 318ms. The Tokyo PoP, which was partially remediated after DRIFT-20260225-001, performed better but still degraded: P50 78ms, P99 189ms. Singapore PoP: P50 94ms, P99 231ms.

Server tick-rate consistency across APAC dropped to 91.4% (competitive threshold: 98.5%). Hit registration accuracy, measured by server-client reconciliation delta, degraded from the normal 2.1ms average to 14.7ms during peak latency, making precise aiming functionally unreliable for competitive play.

## Impact

- **Player experience**: 186,000 APAC players experienced degraded gameplay during a marquee tournament event. The APAC region represents 28% of Eclipse Dominion's global player base and 31% of MTX revenue ($148.8M/yr). Hit registration degradation during a competitive event is considered a "trust-destroying" issue by the competitive community.
- **Esports**: 4 APAC-region semi-professional teams withdrew from Day 3 qualifier matches citing unfair conditions. The APAC qualifier bracket had to be rescheduled to March 6, requiring an additional $45K in broadcast production costs.
- **Financial**: Estimated $1.8M in APAC MTX revenue loss over the 3h42m window (based on APAC hourly MTX baseline of $485K during tournament events, reduced by estimated 100% conversion suppression during severe latency). APAC DAU dropped 8% in the 48 hours following the incident (from 312K to 287K).
- **Competitive integrity**: 890 ranked matches in APAC during the degraded window were flagged for review. 412 were voided after analysis confirmed server-side hit registration failures.
- **Community**: The incident went viral in Japanese and Korean gaming communities. r/EclipseDominion_JP had 2,400 posts in 24 hours (vs. baseline 80/day). Korean streamer "HyunStar" (1.2M followers) posted a clip compilation of hit registration failures that reached 8.3M views.

## Root Cause

The APAC latency spike had two compounding causes:

1. **CDN capacity shortfall**: The emergency capacity scaling from DRIFT-20260302-001 prioritized us-east-1 and eu-west-1 (where 62% of CCU was concentrated). The ap-northeast-1 autoscaler received only 340 of the requested 800 additional instances due to the AWS burst capacity constraint identified on March 2. When APAC prime-time added 186K concurrent players on March 4, the ap-northeast-1 cluster was operating at 94% capacity with no headroom.

2. **CDN configuration fragility (recurrence of DRIFT-20260225-001)**: The CloudEdge CDN configuration fix applied on February 25 addressed the 3 drifted Tokyo nodes but did not audit the Singapore or Sydney PoPs. Under load, the Singapore PoP exhibited the same Osaka-relay routing behavior, adding 35-40ms per hop on 2 of 6 Singapore edge nodes. The Sydney PoP had a separate issue: a stale DNS TTL of 3600s (vs. the intended 300s) was directing 40% of Australian traffic through an intermediate Sydney-Singapore link before reaching the game servers.

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: DRIFT-20260302-001 (CCU surge depleted APAC scaling headroom), DRIFT-20260225-001 (CDN configuration fragility not fully remediated)
- **Downstream**: DRIFT-20260305-001 (competitive fairness complaints -- APAC issues were the primary driver of pro player boycott threat)
- **Decision trigger**: DLR-20260304-001 (emergency APAC infrastructure investment decision)

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate** (07:30-09:30 UTC Mar 4): Manual routing override on Singapore PoP to bypass Osaka relay path. Sydney DNS TTL corrected from 3600s to 300s. Emergency reservation of 460 additional instances in ap-northeast-1 and ap-southeast-1.
2. **Short-term** (Mar 4-5): Full CDN configuration audit across all 24 global PoPs (Tokyo, Singapore, Sydney, Seoul, Mumbai, and 19 others). Identified and remediated 4 additional nodes with routing drift (2 in Seoul, 1 in Mumbai, 1 in Sydney).
3. **APAC infrastructure investment** (Mar 5, per DLR-20260304-001): Approved $2.4M emergency infrastructure spend for APAC region: (a) 600 reserved instances across ap-northeast-1, ap-southeast-1, and ap-southeast-2 for 12 months, (b) dedicated CloudEdge CDN tier with guaranteed burst capacity in Tokyo and Singapore, (c) new Sydney PoP direct-peering agreement with Equinix SY4.
4. **Monitoring** (Mar 5): Deployed per-node, per-PoP latency monitoring at 10-second intervals across all 24 global PoPs (replacing the 15-minute round-robin from pre-DRIFT-20260225-001 era).
5. **Validation**: APAC qualifier reschedule (Mar 6) ran with P50 latency of 52ms and P99 of 71ms across all APAC PoPs. Zero tick-rate violations.
