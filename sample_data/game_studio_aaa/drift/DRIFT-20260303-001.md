## Signal

OrionTel, a Tier-1 sponsor of the Eclipse Dominion Apex Invitational ($8.5M/yr sponsorship, renewed through 2027), flagged a potential breach of their sponsorship agreement clause 7.2 ("Service Quality During Sponsored Events"). Clause 7.2 stipulates that AetherForge must maintain a Sentiment Anomaly Index (SAI) below 75 for any continuous 48-hour period during OrionTel-branded tournament broadcasts. If SAI exceeds 75 for more than 48 consecutive hours, OrionTel may invoke a graduated penalty schedule: 10% fee reduction for 48-72h breach, 25% for 72-96h, and full contract review for >96h.

Following the March 2 CCU surge (DRIFT-20260302-001), the SAI reached 82 at 22:00 UTC on March 2 and remained elevated above 75 for approximately 6 hours before dropping to 71 at 04:00 UTC on March 3. The SAI breach did not reach the 48-hour continuous threshold required to trigger the penalty clause, but OrionTel's brand safety team flagged the spike proactively at 09:15 UTC on March 3, requesting a formal incident report and remediation timeline.

The SAI is a composite index calculated from: Reddit NLP sentiment (30% weight), Twitter/X mention volume and sentiment (25% weight), Discord support ticket velocity (25% weight), and app store review sentiment (20% weight). During the breach window, Reddit r/EclipseDominion had 1,840 negative-sentiment posts (vs. baseline 120/day), Twitter #EclipseDominion mentions hit 94,000 (vs. baseline 12,000/day), and Discord support tickets reached 1,200 in 6 hours (vs. baseline 180/day).

## Impact

- **Sponsorship risk**: OrionTel's $8.5M/yr sponsorship was not technically breached (6h < 48h threshold), but the brand safety team's proactive flag indicates heightened sensitivity. Any subsequent SAI breach during the remaining 4 days of the Apex Invitational could trigger the penalty schedule.
- **Financial exposure**: If clause 7.2 were triggered at the 48-72h tier, the penalty would be $850K (10% of annual fee). At 72-96h tier: $2.125M (25%).
- **Relationship**: OrionTel's CMO personally called AetherForge's VP Partnerships to express concern about brand association during the degraded service period. This is the first time a sponsor has escalated to executive-level contact on a service quality issue.
- **Precedent**: This incident established that SAI can spike rapidly during live events and that sponsor contract thresholds are reachable. This directly informed the VantaFuel crisis 3 days later (DRIFT-20260306-001).

## Root Cause

The SAI spike was a downstream effect of the service degradation caused by DRIFT-20260302-001 (CCU surge) and its cascading impacts on matchmaking (DRIFT-20260302-002). Player frustration with queue times, disconnections, and skill-imbalanced matches drove a rapid sentiment deterioration across all four SAI input channels. The 6-hour duration above 75 reflected the time between the onset of degradation (18:03 UTC Mar 2) and the point at which matchmaking quality returned to baseline (approximately 00:00 UTC Mar 3), plus a 4-hour sentiment decay lag.

The sponsorship contract's SAI clause was negotiated in 2024 when Eclipse Dominion had a peak CCU of 420K and SAI had never exceeded 60. The clause thresholds were not stress-tested against the possibility of a viral-amplified event driving CCU beyond infrastructure capacity.

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: DRIFT-20260302-001 (CCU surge), DRIFT-20260302-002 (matchmaking degradation) drove the sentiment spike
- **Downstream**: DRIFT-20260306-001 (VantaFuel sponsor suspension threat -- VantaFuel uses instantaneous SAI peak, not 48h window)
- **Decision trigger**: Prompted executive review of all sponsor contract SAI clauses (DLR-20260306-004)

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate** (Mar 3, 10:00 UTC): VP Partnerships delivered formal incident report to OrionTel brand safety team, including root cause analysis and the remediation steps already taken for DRIFT-20260302-001.
2. **Short-term** (Mar 3, 14:00 UTC): Implemented real-time SAI monitoring dashboard with alerting thresholds at SAI 60 (warning), 70 (critical), and 75 (sponsor breach imminent). Dashboard accessible to Live Ops, Partnerships, and executive team.
3. **Proactive communication** (Mar 3-4): Scheduled daily 15-minute sync between AetherForge Live Ops and OrionTel brand safety for the remainder of the Apex Invitational.
4. **Contractual** (Mar 10): Legal team initiated review of SAI clause thresholds across all sponsor contracts. Proposed amendment to OrionTel clause 7.2: increase threshold from SAI 75 to SAI 80 for events with CCU >1M (pending OrionTel approval).
5. **Outcome**: OrionTel acknowledged the incident report and did not invoke penalty clauses. SAI remained below 65 for the remainder of the Apex Invitational (Mar 3-6).
