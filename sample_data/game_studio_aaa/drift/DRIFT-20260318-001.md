## Signal

On 2026-03-18, AetherForge's Data Science team completed a model validation review of ASM-0007 ("Churn-Elasticity Model: 10% MTX price increase yields no more than 3% DAU churn in any region"). The review was triggered by the observed churn rates during the W11 monetization crisis, which substantially exceeded the model's predictions.

The churn-elasticity model (v2.1, trained on Season 2-3 data, n=14 pricing events) predicted that the Season 4 EU price increase of 22% would produce approximately 6.6% EU DAU churn over 30 days. Observed churn was 18.4% over 14 days -- 2.8x the model's 30-day prediction, realized in half the time. When scoped to the 10% threshold that ASM-0007 specifically addresses, the model predicted 3.0% churn; back-calculated actual churn at the 10% price delta was 8.2%.

Model diagnostic metrics degraded sharply: R-squared dropped from 0.94 (validation set, Season 3 data) to 0.71 (out-of-sample, W10-W11 data). The model's primary failure mode was its inability to account for compounding negative sentiment -- it treated price sensitivity as independent of concurrent operational failures (tournament degradation, SLA breach, canon contradiction). In reality, churn elasticity is state-dependent: players who are already dissatisfied with service quality churn at 2-3x the rate predicted by price-only models.

## Impact

- **Decision quality**: ASM-0007 was cited as a supporting assumption in 4 active DLRs: DLR-20260224-003 (Season 4 pricing), DLR-20260309-001 (MTX rollback impact estimate), DLR-20260311-001 (loot box conversion revenue projection), and DLR-20260325-001 (pricing harmonization). All four DLRs' financial projections are now unreliable to the extent they depend on the churn-elasticity model.
- **Revenue forecasting**: The EU revenue reforecast (in progress per DRIFT-20260312-001) was using ASM-0007 coefficients. The reforecast must be re-run with updated elasticity parameters, delaying the revised guidance by approximately 5 business days.
- **Assumption chain**: ASM-0007 is referenced by ASM-0012 (ranked fairness assumption, which uses churn-elasticity as an input for player retention modeling). ASM-0012's validity is now uncertain.
- **Process gap**: No automated expiration or validation trigger existed for ASM-0007. The assumption was 4 months past its recommended review date (2025-11-15) when the crisis exposed its failure.

## Root Cause

Three factors caused the model to degrade:

1. **Training data distribution shift**: The model was trained on Season 2-3 pricing events, which occurred during periods of stable service quality and positive community sentiment. The W10-W11 environment (tournament failures, regulatory action, SLA breaches) represents an out-of-distribution regime that the model was never designed to handle.
2. **Independence assumption violation**: The model assumes price sensitivity is independent of other player experience factors. This assumption held during benign conditions (R-squared 0.94) but collapsed under concurrent negative signals (R-squared 0.71).
3. **Missing review cadence**: ASM-0007's recommended review date was 2025-11-15 (90 days after creation). No review was conducted. The assumption management process lacked automated expiration alerts.

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: The model failure was exposed by the W11 monetization crisis ([DRIFT-20260309-001](DRIFT-20260309-001.md) through [DRIFT-20260312-001](DRIFT-20260312-001.md)).
- **Downstream**: Contributed to [DRIFT-20260327-001](DRIFT-20260327-001.md) -- Post-mortem identifying ASM-0007 as one of three invalidated assumptions.
- **Lateral**: [ASM-0012](../intel/provenance/ASM-0012.yaml) (ranked fairness) depends on ASM-0007 coefficients and must be re-validated.

## Patch / Resolution

Resolved on 2026-03-20 via two actions:

1. **Model retirement and replacement**: ASM-0007 v2.1 formally retired. A replacement model (v3.0) was commissioned with the following changes: (a) state-dependent elasticity -- churn predictions conditioned on a "sentiment health" composite score derived from NPS, SAI, and support ticket volume; (b) training data expanded to include W10-W11 events; (c) validation protocol requires monthly backtesting against trailing 90-day observed data. v3.0 target delivery: 2026-04-08.
2. **Assumption management automation**: DLR-20260320-002 established automated assumption expiration alerts. All ASM records now have a hard expiry date and a 14-day pre-expiry warning sent to the assumption owner and all DLRs that reference the assumption. Retroactive scan identified 3 additional assumptions past their review dates (ASM-0003, ASM-0011, ASM-0015).
