## Signal

On March 9 at 10:00 UTC, AetherForge Interactive received a formal regulatory notice from the Belgian Gaming Commission (Kansspelcommissie) requiring submission of a compliance plan for Eclipse Dominion's loot box mechanics within 60 calendar days (deadline: May 8, 2026). The notice specifically cites Eclipse Dominion's "Dominion Crates" system, which allows players to purchase randomized cosmetic item bundles at price points of EUR 2.49, EUR 4.99, and EUR 9.99, with published drop rates.

The Belgian notice references the 2018 Belgian Gaming Commission ruling that classified certain loot box mechanics as gambling, and states that Eclipse Dominion's Dominion Crates "present characteristics substantially similar to games of chance" under Belgian law. The notice demands: (1) a detailed technical description of the randomization system, (2) disclosure of all drop rate tables and pity timer mechanics, (3) a compliance remediation plan, or (4) voluntary withdrawal of the Dominion Crates system from the Belgian market.

Compounding the Belgian notice, a leaked draft guidance document from the Dutch Kansspelautoriteit (Netherlands Gaming Authority) was published on March 8 by NRC Handelsblad. The leaked document proposes new classification criteria for "paid randomized reward mechanics" in games rated PEGI 12 and above, which would encompass Eclipse Dominion (rated PEGI 16). If adopted, the Dutch guidance would require either: (a) removal of paid randomized mechanics, (b) age-gating with national ID verification, or (c) reclassification under Dutch gambling licensing (estimated compliance cost: EUR 2-4M annually plus ongoing regulatory reporting).

The Berlin studio's regulatory monitoring also flagged that the European Parliament's Committee on Internal Market and Consumer Protection (IMCO) scheduled a hearing on "Monetization Practices in Digital Entertainment" for April 22, 2026, with Eclipse Dominion named in the hearing agenda as a case study.

## Impact

- **Financial (EU monetization)**: Eclipse Dominion's EU MTX revenue is $134.4M/yr (28% of global $480M). Dominion Crates represent approximately 38% of EU MTX revenue ($51.1M/yr). If loot boxes are banned or restricted across Belgium and the Netherlands (combined: 8.2% of EU player base), direct revenue impact is estimated at $4.2M/yr. If the regulatory trend extends to Germany, France, and broader EU via IMCO action, exposure rises to $51.1M/yr (full EU loot box revenue).
- **Legal costs**: Outside counsel engagement for Belgian compliance response estimated at EUR 280K. Dutch regulatory monitoring and contingency planning: EUR 150K. IMCO hearing preparation: EUR 95K.
- **Platform risk**: Apple App Store and Google Play guidelines require compliance with local gambling regulations. Non-compliance with Belgian or Dutch rulings could result in Eclipse Dominion removal from app stores in those territories.
- **Precedent**: This is the first formal regulatory action specifically targeting Eclipse Dominion's monetization. The outcome will set a precedent for AetherForge's other titles and for the broader live-service game industry.
- **Timeline pressure**: The 60-day Belgian deadline (May 8) coincides with the planned Season 5 launch, which includes a major Dominion Crates refresh. Any compliance-driven changes to the crate system must be factored into Season 5 development, affecting the Berlin and LA studios' roadmaps.

## Root Cause

Eclipse Dominion's Dominion Crates system was designed in 2024 based on industry-standard loot box mechanics with published drop rates, a pity timer guaranteeing a legendary item within 30 opens, and no gameplay-affecting items (cosmetic only). At launch, AetherForge's legal team assessed the system as compliant with Belgian law based on a 2023 enforcement memo suggesting cosmetic-only loot boxes were lower enforcement priority.

However, three factors converged to trigger the regulatory action:

1. **Revenue growth**: Dominion Crates revenue in Belgium grew 180% year-over-year (from EUR 1.8M in 2024 to EUR 5.0M in 2025), driven by the Apex Invitational's popularity. The growth attracted regulatory attention.
2. **Political pressure**: Belgian MP Kathleen Verhelst (Open VLD) cited Eclipse Dominion by name in a February 2026 parliamentary question about "predatory monetization targeting young gamers," generating media coverage in De Standaard and Het Laatste Nieuws.
3. **Enforcement shift**: The Belgian Gaming Commission's 2026 enforcement priorities (published January 2026) explicitly included "high-revenue randomized digital content systems" as a focus area, reversing the 2023 deprioritization of cosmetic-only loot boxes.

The Dutch leak appears to be part of a coordinated EU-wide regulatory movement. AetherForge's Berlin regulatory affairs team had flagged the trend in their Q4 2025 risk assessment but the recommended proactive compliance review was deprioritized due to budget constraints.

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate legal response** (Mar 9, 14:00 UTC): Engaged Baker McKenzie Brussels office as outside counsel for Belgian compliance response. 48-hour preliminary assessment commissioned.
2. **Regulatory engagement** (Mar 10): Berlin VP Legal Affairs contacted Belgian Gaming Commission to request a preliminary meeting (offered March 18). Objective: understand the specific mechanics objected to and explore remediation options short of full withdrawal.
3. **Dutch monitoring** (Mar 10): Engaged Houthoff (Amsterdam) to monitor the Kansspelautoriteit guidance process and prepare a comment submission during the public consultation period (expected April 2026).
4. **IMCO preparation** (Mar 11): Retained FTI Consulting for IMCO hearing preparation. AetherForge will submit a written position paper and has requested a speaking slot to present the "responsible randomization" framework.
5. **Technical contingency** (Mar 11, per DLR-20260311-001): Berlin studio began developing a "direct purchase" alternative to Dominion Crates for EU markets. Design spec: players can purchase specific cosmetics at fixed prices (2-3x the expected-value price of the crate equivalent). Target: testable build by April 15, production-ready by April 30 (8 days before the May 8 Belgian deadline).
6. **Evidence preservation** (Mar 9): Internal legal hold issued per EVID-0003 (evidence preservation protocol). All Dominion Crates analytics data, drop rate configurations, revenue data, and internal communications preserved.
7. **Status as of Mar 20**: Belgian preliminary meeting scheduled for March 18 (pending). Dutch public consultation not yet opened. IMCO hearing prep on track. Direct-purchase alternative in design phase. Resolution timeline: 60-90 days.
