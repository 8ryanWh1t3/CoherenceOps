# DRIFT-20260323-001: Recovery Metrics Below Target: D7 Retention

## Signal

AetherForge's Player Insights team reported that EU D7 retention (the percentage of players who return to Eclipse Dominion on day 7 after their most recent session) has stalled at 38%, falling short of the 42% recovery target established in the W12 remediation plan. The 42% target was itself a compromise -- pre-crisis EU D7 retention was 47% (W09 baseline). The current 38% represents a 9-point deficit from pre-crisis levels and a 4-point miss against the recovery plan.

The D7 metric is a leading indicator for monthly active user (MAU) trajectories. At 38% D7 retention, the EU MAU model projects a stabilization at approximately 312,000 monthly actives by W16 -- 14% below the pre-crisis EU MAU of 362,000 and 8% below the recovery target of 340,000. Each MAU point in EU represents approximately $1.2M in annualized revenue at current ARPPU levels.

Cohort analysis reveals a bifurcated recovery pattern: (1) players who were active during the tournament spike (W10) and experienced the monetization backlash directly have a D7 retention of 31% -- these players carry a "scar" effect from the pricing controversy; (2) players who joined or returned after the crisis (W12+) have a D7 retention of 48% -- above the pre-crisis baseline. The scarred cohort represents 64% of the EU DAU base and is dragging the overall D7 metric down.

Weekly improvement has been slow: D7 retention moved from 35% (W11 trough) to 36% (W12) to 38% (W13). At this trajectory (+1.5 points/week), the 42% target will not be reached until W16 -- two weeks later than the remediation plan's W14 target.

## Impact

- **Revenue**: The 4-point D7 retention miss translates to approximately 22,000 fewer monthly returning players than planned. At current EU ARPPU of $8.90 (still depressed per DRIFT-20260312-001), this represents -$2.3M annualized revenue shortfall beyond the ARPPU collapse impact already accounted for.
- **Recovery timeline**: The EU recovery plan (established post-DLR-20260309-001) assumed D7 retention would reach 42% by W14 and 45% by W18. Current trajectory pushes these milestones to W16 and W21 respectively, extending the recovery window by 6 weeks.
- **Earnings guidance**: The Q1 earnings call (2026-04-15) will need to address the extended recovery timeline. If D7 retention does not reach 42% by the earnings call, the CFO will likely need to revise FY2026 EU revenue guidance downward by $8-12M.
- **Sponsor negotiations**: The stalled recovery compounds DRIFT-20260313-002 (sponsor renegotiation). OrionTel's counter-offer includes DAU recovery to >95% baseline as a condition for rate restoration. At current trajectory, >95% DAU recovery is not achievable before Q3.

## Root Cause

The below-target D7 retention is driven by the "scarred cohort" effect -- a behavioral pattern where players who personally experienced a trust-eroding event exhibit persistently lower engagement even after the triggering issue is resolved. Contributing factors:

1. **Trust deficit persistence**: The pricing controversy (Season 4 price increase followed by emergency rollback) created a "fool me once" effect. Scarred-cohort players who do return spend 23% less time per session and visit the storefront 41% less frequently than their pre-crisis behavior.
2. **Alternative game uptake**: During the W11 DAU decline, EU players migrated to competing titles. Telemetry from PlayStation cross-play APIs shows 34% of lapsed EU players logged sessions in Apex Legends or Valorant during W11-W12. Re-acquisition from established competitor engagement is historically slow (8-12 week cycle).
3. **Content cadence impact**: The QA bottleneck (DRIFT-20260319-001) has slowed the content patch cadence, reducing the "new content pull" that typically drives re-engagement. Season 4.1's Battle Pass content update, originally scheduled for W12, slipped to W13 due to QA queue delays.

## Patch / Resolution

**Status: Open.** No single action resolves the scarred-cohort retention gap. Multi-track recovery strategy:

1. **Scarred-cohort re-engagement campaign** (Berlin, launched 2026-03-22): Targeted in-game messaging and email outreach to the scarred cohort (identified by W10-W11 session overlap with monetization event exposure). Messaging emphasizes CANON-v1.3 pricing commitments and offers a "Welcome Back" cosmetic bundle (free, exclusive). Early signal: 8% of targeted players returned within 48 hours of message receipt.
2. **Content acceleration** (Montreal, in progress): Season 4.1 mid-season event ("Crimson Rift") pulled forward from W15 to W14 to provide a content-driven re-engagement hook. Requires QA prioritization (coordinated with Warsaw per DRIFT-20260319-001 triage protocol).
3. **Competitive integrity showcase** (LA Esports, scheduled W14): Community tournament with transparent ranked-integrity reporting (per DLR-20260304-002) to rebuild competitive trust. Prize pool: $50K. Target viewership: 200K.

Target: D7 retention to 42% by W16 (revised from W14). Full resolution (return to >45% baseline) requires sustained execution through W21.
