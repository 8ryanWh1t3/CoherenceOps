## Signal

Between March 3 00:00 UTC and March 5 00:00 UTC, AetherForge received 4,200 support tickets tagged "competitive fairness" -- a 2,800% increase over the baseline of 150 tickets per 48-hour period. The ticket surge was concentrated in three categories: hit registration failures (1,840 tickets, 43.8%), matchmaking skill imbalance (1,520 tickets, 36.2%), and disconnection during ranked matches (840 tickets, 20.0%).

The geographic distribution of complaints was heavily APAC-skewed: 58% from APAC (2,436 tickets), 24% from EU (1,008), and 18% from NA (756). This aligns with the APAC latency degradation documented in DRIFT-20260304-001 being the most severe regional impact.

Most critically, 14 professional esports players with a combined social following of 18.6M issued a joint open letter on March 4 at 22:00 UTC threatening to boycott the Apex Invitational finals (scheduled March 7-8) unless AetherForge published a transparency report on server conditions and committed to compensating affected matches. The letter was posted on Twitlonger and amplified by esports journalists at Dexerto, Dot Esports, and TheGamer within 2 hours, generating 340 media mentions in 24 hours.

Three APAC-region esports organizations (T1, DRX, and ZETA DIVISION) issued formal statements supporting the boycott threat and requesting a meeting with AetherForge's esports division.

## Impact

- **Esports**: The boycott threat endangered the Apex Invitational finals, the highest-profile competitive event in Eclipse Dominion history (projected 2.4M peak viewers). If the top 14 pros withdrew, the event would lose broadcast credibility and approximately $3.2M in sponsorship activation value.
- **Financial**: The media coverage drove a secondary SAI spike (see DRIFT-20260305-002). Combined with the original tournament degradation, estimated total revenue impact from the fairness complaints and threatened boycott: $5.8M (including $3.2M sponsorship activation risk, $1.8M MTX suppression from APAC latency, and $800K in customer support operational costs for processing 4,200 tickets at $190/ticket average handling cost).
- **Player trust**: Net Promoter Score (NPS) for Eclipse Dominion dropped from +42 to +18 in the daily rolling survey (March 5 sample, n=12,400). The -24 point NPS drop is the largest single-event decline in the game's history.
- **Community**: r/EclipseDominion subreddit upvoted a "State of the Game" criticism post to 34,200 upvotes (all-time #1 post). Discord community manager team reported 14 hours of sustained hostile engagement across 4 official channels.

## Root Cause

The competitive fairness complaints were the aggregate player response to three cascading service degradations during the Apex Invitational:

1. **Matchmaking skill imbalance** (DRIFT-20260302-002): The fallback matcher's 340 SR average differential during the March 2 degradation window produced 2,100 unfair matches that directly impacted player rankings.
2. **APAC hit registration failures** (DRIFT-20260304-001): 14.7ms server-client reconciliation delta (vs. 2.1ms normal) made competitive play unreliable for 186,000 APAC players over 3h 42m.
3. **Match voiding delays**: The 1,559 matches flagged for review (312 from DRIFT-20260302-002 + 412 from DRIFT-20260304-001 + 835 additional) took 48+ hours to process, leaving affected players in ranking limbo.

The pro player boycott threat was catalyzed by APAC team DRX's qualifier loss in a match with documented 189ms P99 latency, which DRX argued was unplayable for competitive FPS. The loss eliminated DRX from the Invitational bracket, converting a technical issue into a competitive-integrity crisis.

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: DRIFT-20260304-001 (APAC latency -- primary driver), DRIFT-20260302-002 (matchmaking quality degradation)
- **Downstream**: DRIFT-20260305-002 (SAI spike to 82 from media amplification of boycott threat)
- **Decision trigger**: DLR-20260304-002 (APAC qualifier reschedule), DLR-20260306-003 (competitive integrity remediation plan)

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate community response** (Mar 5, 06:00 UTC): Community Director published "State of Eclipse Dominion Competitive Integrity" blog post acknowledging all three service degradation events, providing a technical timeline, and committing to a remediation plan. The post was co-signed by AetherForge's CEO and CTO.
2. **Pro player engagement** (Mar 5, 10:00 UTC): VP Esports held a 90-minute video call with the 14 boycott-threatening pros and representatives from T1, DRX, and ZETA DIVISION. Agreed terms: (a) DRX qualifier match replayed under verified server conditions, (b) all voided matches processed within 24 hours, (c) $50K player compensation pool for affected semi-professional players, (d) public server health dashboard launching before finals.
3. **Match remediation** (Mar 5-6): Retroactive analysis team (12 engineers, Warsaw + Tokyo) processed all 1,559 flagged matches within 18 hours. 724 voided, SR adjustments applied to 4,812 affected players. Average SR correction: +/- 34 points.
4. **Server health dashboard** (Mar 6): Public-facing real-time dashboard launched at dashboard.eclipsedominion.com showing per-region latency, tick-rate, and matchmaking quality metrics. First in the competitive FPS genre to offer this level of transparency.
5. **Outcome**: All 14 pros confirmed participation in the Apex Invitational finals. DRX won their replayed qualifier match. Boycott threat resolved. NPS recovered to +31 by March 10 (still below +42 pre-incident baseline).
