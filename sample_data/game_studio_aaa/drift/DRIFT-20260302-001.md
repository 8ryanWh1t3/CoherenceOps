## Signal

The W10 Apex Invitational tournament launch on March 2 produced a concurrent user (CCU) peak of 1.15 million at 18:07 UTC, exceeding the pre-event projection of 620K CCU by 285% (effectively 340% of the infrastructure provisioned capacity of 338K with autoscaler headroom to 750K). This is the highest CCU in Eclipse Dominion history, surpassing the Season 3 launch peak of 740K by 55%.

The Kubernetes autoscaler detected the capacity breach at 17:58 UTC when pod utilization crossed the 85% threshold, but the scaling response lagged by 9 minutes due to cloud provider instance provisioning delays (AWS requested burst capacity of 2,400 additional c6g.4xlarge instances across us-east-1, eu-west-1, and ap-northeast-1; actual delivery was staggered over 9 minutes with ap-northeast-1 last at 18:07 UTC). During the 9-minute scaling gap, server utilization hit 97.3% and multiple downstream systems degraded.

The projection miss originated from the analytics team's CCU model, which was trained on Season 1-3 tournament data (peak: 740K) and did not account for three compounding factors: (1) a viral TikTok campaign by pro player "Kael_X" (42M views in 48h), (2) a Twitch Drops integration that launched 6 hours before the tournament, and (3) simultaneous promotion by 3 hardware partners (NVIDIA, Razer, SteelSeries) offering in-game cosmetics for viewers.

## Impact

- **Player experience**: During the 9-minute scaling gap, approximately 340,000 players experienced degraded service including matchmaking failures, rubber-banding, and disconnections. An estimated 82,000 players were unable to connect at all. Post-scaling, service quality recovered but APAC remained degraded (see DRIFT-20260304-001).
- **Financial**: Estimated $2.1M in lost microtransaction revenue during the 9-minute degradation window (based on average hourly MTX revenue of $14M/hr during comparable peak events, reduced by estimated 85% conversion suppression). Additional $380K in emergency cloud compute burst charges.
- **Sponsorship**: OrionTel and VantaFuel sponsor contracts contain uptime and sentiment clauses that were triggered (see DRIFT-20260303-001, DRIFT-20260306-001). Combined contractual risk exposure: $4.8M.
- **Competitive integrity**: 1,247 ranked competitive matches were affected during the scaling gap. 312 matches were voided and required replay adjudication.

## Root Cause

The CCU projection model used by the analytics team (Model: ED-CCU-v2.1, last retrained November 2025) was based on historical tournament data without accounting for exogenous viral amplification factors. The model's 95% confidence interval topped out at 820K CCU. The three compounding amplification factors (TikTok virality, Twitch Drops, hardware partner promotions) were managed by separate teams (Community, Partnerships, and Marketing respectively) with no unified demand signal aggregation.

The autoscaler configuration (HPA v2beta2, scaling policy: 120s stabilization window, 200% max surge) was adequate for organic growth patterns but insufficient for the step-function demand increase. The 9-minute lag was primarily caused by AWS instance provisioning in ap-northeast-1, which had reduced burst capacity due to a concurrent large-scale deployment by another customer in the same availability zone.

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate** (18:07-18:22 UTC Mar 2): AWS burst capacity fully provisioned. All regions stabilized. Manual override of autoscaler to maintain 150% headroom above observed peak.
2. **Short-term** (Mar 2-3): Pre-provisioned 3,200 reserved instances across all regions for the remainder of the Apex Invitational (incremental cost: $142K for 5-day reservation).
3. **Medium-term** (Mar 5): CCU projection model retrained with viral amplification coefficients. New model (ED-CCU-v3.0) includes real-time social media velocity signals from TikTok, Twitch, and Twitter as leading indicators. Backtested against the March 2 event, v3.0 predicted 1.08M CCU (6% error vs. v2.1's 46% error).
4. **Process** (Mar 6): Established cross-functional "Demand Signal Council" with representatives from Analytics, Community, Partnerships, and Marketing. Council meets 72h before any major event to aggregate demand signals.
5. **Infrastructure** (Mar 8): Autoscaler policy updated: stabilization window reduced from 120s to 45s, max surge increased from 200% to 400%, and multi-region warm pool of 800 standby instances deployed.
6. **Validation**: Apex Invitational Day 3 (Mar 4) peaked at 980K CCU with zero scaling lag.
