# DRIFT-20260317-001: Cross-Region Pricing Parity Drift NA vs EU

## Signal

Berlin's Revenue Analytics team identified a significant cross-region pricing parity drift between NA and EU storefronts following the EU MTX rollback (DLR-20260309-001) and the kill switch incident (DRIFT-20260316-001). The EU storefront is now operating on Season 3 pricing (the `eu-mtx-s3-final` snapshot), while the NA storefront remains on Season 4 pricing. This creates a 22% average price gap across 47 comparable SKUs, with individual SKU deltas ranging from 12% to 31%.

The parity drift was first surfaced by community data miners who published a side-by-side pricing comparison spreadsheet on the Eclipse Dominion subreddit (142,000 upvotes, 8,400 comments). Players identified that using a VPN to access the EU storefront yields substantial savings on identical items. Within 48 hours of the post, VPN usage from NA IP ranges accessing the EU storefront increased 680% (from ~200 sessions/day to ~1,560 sessions/day based on CDN geolocation logs).

Grey-market arbitrage has also emerged: third-party key resellers are purchasing EU-priced bundles and reselling them to NA players at a 10-15% markup over EU price (still 7-12% below NA price). AetherForge's anti-fraud team detected 340 suspected arbitrage accounts in the first 72 hours, with an estimated $28,000 in arbitrage volume.

## Impact

- **Revenue leakage**: At current VPN bypass rates, estimated NA revenue leakage of $180K/month. If VPN workaround awareness grows (likely given viral subreddit post), leakage could reach $420K/month by W16.
- **Player sentiment**: NA players perceive the price gap as unfair. NA player NPS for the "value for money" segment dropped 4 points (from 38 to 34) in the 72 hours following the subreddit post. EU players, conversely, are satisfied with lower prices but concerned about future increases.
- **Arbitrage risk**: Grey-market resale undermines AetherForge's storefront integrity and creates customer support burden (disputed transactions, chargebacks). Estimated chargeback risk: $12K/month from arbitrage-related disputes.
- **Regulatory complexity**: Any NA price reduction to restore parity would reduce global revenue. Any EU price increase risks re-triggering the DSC notice (DRIFT-20260309-001). AetherForge is caught in a pricing equilibrium trap.

## Root Cause

The pricing parity drift is a direct structural consequence of the EU-only MTX rollback (DLR-20260309-001). The rollback was scoped exclusively to EU markets to minimize global revenue impact and respond to EU-specific regulatory pressure. No parity review was conducted at the time of rollback because the decision was made under sev-1 emergency conditions with a 6-hour execution window. The pricing team's assumption -- that regional price differences would not be widely noticed -- was invalidated by the community data mining post within 8 days.

## Patch / Resolution

**Status: In Progress.** Three-phase harmonization plan under development:

1. **Phase 1 -- Arbitrage mitigation** (deployed 2026-03-19): Geo-fencing enhancement to block VPN-detected sessions from completing EU storefront purchases when the session originates from a non-EU IP. 92% effective based on CDN-level detection. Remaining 8% (sophisticated VPN users) accepted as residual risk.
2. **Phase 2 -- NA price adjustment** (scheduled 2026-03-25, per DLR-20260325-001): Selective NA price reduction on the 12 highest-delta SKUs (those with >25% gap). Reduces average parity gap from 22% to 14%. Estimated NA revenue impact: -$1.8M/yr. Requires 14-day advance notice per CANON-v1.3 pricing transparency commitment.
3. **Phase 3 -- PPP-adjusted global pricing** (target: Season 5 launch, 2026-05-01): Full purchasing-power-parity-adjusted pricing model across all regions. Eliminates structural parity drift. Requires new pricing engine (estimated build cost: $340K). DLR pending.

Full resolution expected by Season 5 launch. Current parity gap will narrow to ~14% by W14 and persist at that level until Phase 3.
