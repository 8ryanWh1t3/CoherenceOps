# DRIFT-20260325-001: Residual Pricing Parity Drift LATAM

## Signal

During the W13 pricing parity audit (initiated as part of the cross-region pricing harmonization effort following DRIFT-20260317-001), Berlin's Revenue Analytics team discovered that LATAM pricing was not adjusted in the Season 4.1 cosmetic rebalance deployed on 2026-03-18. The cosmetic rebalance (per DLR-20260311-001) restructured 23 loot-box SKUs into direct-purchase bundles and recalibrated pricing for the EU storefront. NA pricing received selective adjustments in the Phase 2 harmonization (DLR-20260325-001). LATAM pricing was not included in either adjustment.

The result is an 18% average gap between LATAM prices and their purchasing-power-parity (PPP) adjusted equivalence. LATAM prices are set in USD at the same nominal values as NA (Season 4 pricing), despite LATAM PPP-adjusted purchasing power being approximately 45% lower than NA. Before Season 4, LATAM had a 12% PPP gap (inherited from the launch pricing model, which used a simplified 3-tier regional pricing structure). The Season 4 price increase widened the gap to 18% because the percentage increase was applied uniformly across regions without PPP adjustment.

LATAM represents 8% of Eclipse Dominion's global DAU (approximately 56,000 daily players) and 4.8% of global revenue ($23M/yr at plan). LATAM ARPPU is $6.80 -- the lowest of any region -- and has been declining 2.1% month-over-month since Season 4 launch, suggesting price sensitivity erosion.

## Impact

- **Revenue at risk**: The 18% PPP gap suggests LATAM MTX conversion rates are suppressed by an estimated 12-15% relative to PPP-optimized pricing. At current LATAM revenue of $23M/yr, PPP-adjusted pricing could yield an incremental $2.8-3.5M/yr in LATAM MTX revenue (based on Berlin's price-elasticity model for emerging markets, validated on APAC data).
- **Player equity**: LATAM players are paying a higher effective price (as a percentage of disposable income) than any other region. This creates a fairness perception issue, particularly visible in cross-region communities where LATAM players compare prices with EU players (who benefited from the rollback).
- **Churn risk**: LATAM D7 retention is 40% (vs 43% global average), and the LATAM churn model shows a 0.8% DAU sensitivity to each 5% of PPP gap. The 18% gap implies approximately 2.9% incremental DAU churn attributable to overpricing -- roughly 1,600 players/month.
- **Low severity rationale**: While the PPP gap is material, LATAM revenue contribution is relatively small (4.8% of global) and the pricing issue is structural rather than crisis-driven. No regulatory or sponsor exposure.

## Root Cause

LATAM was excluded from the pricing adjustments for two reasons:

1. **Scope limitation**: The EU MTX rollback (DLR-20260309-001) was scoped exclusively to EU to address EU-specific regulatory pressure. The NA Phase 2 adjustment (DLR-20260325-001) was scoped to NA to address the highest-visibility parity gap (NA vs EU, per the viral subreddit post). LATAM was deprioritized because it had no regulatory trigger and no viral community attention.
2. **PPP pricing absence**: AetherForge does not have a PPP-adjusted pricing engine. Regional pricing has been set using a simplified 3-tier model (Tier 1: NA/EU/APAC-developed; Tier 2: LATAM/APAC-emerging; Tier 3: not used). The 3-tier model does not granularly adjust for country-level PPP within a tier. Building a PPP engine is part of the Phase 3 pricing harmonization (target: Season 5 launch), but LATAM will not benefit until that system is deployed.

## Patch / Resolution

**Status: Open.** LATAM pricing adjustment is queued for Phase 3 of the global pricing harmonization plan:

1. **Interim measure** (under evaluation): Apply a flat 10% LATAM price reduction to the 10 highest-revenue SKUs. This would narrow the PPP gap from 18% to approximately 10% at a cost of -$1.1M/yr in LATAM revenue. Decision pending -- Berlin's Revenue Analytics team is modeling the elasticity impact to determine if the volume uplift offsets the per-unit price reduction.
2. **Phase 3 resolution** (target: Season 5 launch, 2026-05-01): Full PPP-adjusted pricing engine deployment. LATAM pricing will be set at country-level PPP-adjusted rates. Estimated build cost for PPP engine: $340K (shared with NA/EU Phase 3 work). LATAM-specific implementation: $45K additional for country-level data integration (Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia as priority markets).

No target resolution date set for interim measure. Phase 3 target: 2026-05-01.
