# DRIFT-20260302-002: Matchmaking Queue Time Spike (cascade from 302-001)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Severity** | high |
| **Status** | patched |
| **Detected** | 2026-03-02 18:12 UTC |
| **Reporter** | Matchmaking Systems Lead, Warsaw Studio |
| **Studio** | Warsaw |
| **Region** | Global |

## Signal

Matchmaking queue times spiked to 204 seconds at P95 during the Apex Invitational launch window, against a normal baseline of 12 seconds P95. The spike began at 18:03 UTC, approximately 5 minutes after the CCU surge overwhelmed provisioned capacity (see DRIFT-20260302-001), and persisted for 34 minutes until 18:37 UTC. P50 queue times peaked at 87 seconds (baseline: 4 seconds).

Approximately 253,000 unique players (22% of the 1.15M CCU peak) experienced queue times exceeding 60 seconds during the affected window. Of these, an estimated 41,000 players abandoned the queue entirely (abandon rate: 16.2%, vs. normal 1.8%). The matchmaking service (ELO-Match v4.2, maintained by the Warsaw studio) entered a degraded state when its Redis-backed session cache exceeded 92% memory utilization, causing the skill-based matching algorithm to fall back to a simplified bracket matcher with wider skill tolerance bands.

The fallback matcher successfully prevented total matchmaking failure but produced lower-quality matches: average skill differential in matches formed during the degraded window was 340 SR (Skill Rating) points, compared to the normal target of 80 SR. This directly contributed to the competitive fairness complaints documented in DRIFT-20260305-001.

## Impact

- **Player experience**: 253,000 players experienced extended queue times. 41,000 abandoned queues (lost sessions). Players who did match were placed in skill-imbalanced games (340 SR avg differential vs. 80 SR target), producing a poor competitive experience.
- **Financial**: Estimated $640K in lost MTX revenue from queue abandonment (41K players x avg session MTX spend of $15.60 during tournament events). Additional downstream impact from churn: 3,800 players who experienced queue abandonment did not return within 72 hours (vs. expected 400 for normal churn).
- **Competitive integrity**: 2,100 ranked matches formed during the degraded window had skill differentials exceeding 200 SR. Tournament qualifier standings were disputed by 18 semi-professional teams.
- **Esports**: Two broadcast commentators noted on-stream that "matchmaking seems broken" to an audience of 380,000 concurrent Twitch viewers, amplifying negative sentiment.

## Root Cause

The matchmaking service's Redis session cache was provisioned for a maximum of 800K concurrent sessions based on the pre-event CCU projection of 620K (with 30% headroom). When CCU hit 1.15M, the cache exceeded its 92% memory threshold at 18:03 UTC, triggering the automatic fallback to the simplified bracket matcher. The fallback matcher is designed as a circuit-breaker to prevent total matchmaking failure, but it trades match quality for throughput.

The 9-minute autoscaler lag from DRIFT-20260302-001 compounded the issue: even as game server capacity was restored at 18:07 UTC, the matchmaking Redis cluster required an additional 30 minutes to rebalance and drain the backlogged queue (approximately 180,000 pending match requests at peak backlog).

## Cascade Chain

- **Upstream**: Directly caused by DRIFT-20260302-001 (CCU surge exceeding capacity)
- **Downstream**: Skill-imbalanced matches during degraded window contributed to DRIFT-20260305-001 (competitive fairness complaints) and DRIFT-20260305-002 (SAI spike)
- **Decision trigger**: DLR-20260306-003 (matchmaking infrastructure scaling decision)

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate** (18:37 UTC Mar 2): Redis session cache stabilized after autoscaled game servers absorbed the CCU load. Queue times returned to 18s P95 by 18:45 UTC and baseline 12s by 19:10 UTC.
2. **Short-term** (Mar 3): Redis cluster scaled from 6 to 12 nodes with 2TB total memory (vs. previous 1TB). New capacity ceiling: 1.8M concurrent sessions.
3. **Match quality remediation** (Mar 3-4): Warsaw team ran retroactive analysis on 2,100 skill-imbalanced matches. 312 ranked matches with >400 SR differential were voided, and affected players received SR adjustment credits.
4. **Medium-term** (Mar 6): Matchmaking fallback matcher updated to enforce a 150 SR maximum differential (previously unbounded in fallback mode). New fallback mode trades queue time for match quality -- estimated P95 in degraded mode now 300s with 150 SR cap vs. previous 204s with no cap.
5. **Validation**: Apex Invitational Day 3 (Mar 4) with 980K CCU: queue times peaked at 14s P95, zero fallback activations.
