# DRIFT-20260327-001: Post-Mortem: 3 Assumptions Invalidated

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Severity** | high |
| **Status** | open |
| **Detected** | 2026-03-27 |
| **Reporter** | VP Engineering (LA) |
| **Studio** | LA (all studios consulted) |

## Signal

The W13 cross-studio retrospective (held 2026-03-27, all five studios represented) formally concluded that three foundational assumptions underlying Eclipse Dominion's operational and business planning were invalidated during the W09-W13 period. The three assumptions had been flagged individually in earlier drift signals but the retrospective consolidated them into a single post-mortem finding with systemic implications.

**ASM-0005 -- Tournament CCU Ceiling**: "Peak concurrent users will not exceed 2x baseline during live events." Actual: 3.4x baseline during the Eclipse Dominion Invitational (W10). The assumption was based on Season 2-3 tournament data where peak CCU never exceeded 1.7x baseline. The W10 event's viral social media moment (a record-breaking 47-kill streak by pro player "VoidHex") drove unprecedented organic viewership-to-player conversion. ASM-0005 was the root assumption behind the auto-scaling ceiling that triggered the W10 infrastructure cascade (DRIFT-20260302-001).

**ASM-0007 -- Churn-Elasticity Model**: "A 10% MTX price increase yields no more than 3% DAU churn in any region." Actual: 8.2% DAU churn per 10% price increase under concurrent negative-sentiment conditions. The model's R-squared degraded from 0.94 to 0.71. ASM-0007 was a supporting assumption for 4 DLRs including the Season 4 pricing strategy that triggered the monetization crisis. Detailed in DRIFT-20260318-001.

**ASM-0012 -- Ranked Fairness Perception**: "Competitive players will tolerate up to 72 hours of known balance issues without measurable engagement impact, provided a fix timeline is communicated." Actual: Engagement decline began within 18 hours of the Void Reaper imbalance being publicly reported, even though AetherForge communicated a 48-hour fix timeline within 6 hours of detection. Competitive player session length dropped 28% within 24 hours and ranked queue participation declined 19% within 36 hours. The 72-hour tolerance window was based on a Season 2 survey (n=2,400) that asked players about hypothetical scenarios -- observed behavior under real conditions showed tolerance was approximately 4x shorter than stated preferences.

The retrospective rated the systemic finding as high severity because the three invalidated assumptions collectively underpinned $52M in annual revenue-dependent decisions and the company's entire live-event operational framework.

## Impact

- **Decision integrity**: 11 active DLRs reference at least one of the three invalidated assumptions. These DLRs contain financial projections, capacity plans, and SLA commitments that are now built on invalid foundations. A full DLR audit is required to identify which decisions need revision.
- **Planning framework**: AetherForge's annual planning model uses ASM-0005 (capacity), ASM-0007 (revenue elasticity), and ASM-0012 (engagement resilience) as baseline parameters. The FY2026 operating plan was built on these assumptions. The CFO has requested a reforecast by 2026-04-10 with updated parameters.
- **Assumption management process**: The retrospective identified that AetherForge had no systematic process for assumption validation, expiration, or cascade-impact analysis. Assumptions were created as point-in-time records and referenced in DLRs but never re-validated against observed data until a crisis forced the issue.
- **Organizational learning**: The pattern of "assumptions validated under benign conditions fail under stress" suggests that AetherForge's planning process has a systematic optimism bias. The retrospective recommended stress-testing all assumptions against adversarial scenarios, not just baseline scenarios.

## Root Cause

The systemic root cause is the absence of an assumption lifecycle management process:

1. **No expiration enforcement**: ASM-0005 was created 2025-08-14 with a recommended review date of 2025-11-14. It was never reviewed. ASM-0007 was created 2025-07-22 with a recommended review of 2025-11-15. Also never reviewed. ASM-0012 was created 2025-09-01 with a recommended review of 2025-12-01. Also never reviewed. All three assumptions were 4-6 months past their review dates when they failed.
2. **No stress-testing protocol**: Assumptions were validated against historical data from benign operating periods. No process existed to test assumptions against concurrent-failure scenarios, competitive shocks, or regulatory actions.
3. **No cascade-impact mapping**: When ASM-0005 was invalidated by the W10 CCU spike, there was no automated mechanism to flag all DLRs that depended on it. The cascade had to be traced manually during the crisis, consuming leadership bandwidth that should have been directed at resolution.

## Patch / Resolution

**Status: Open.** The retrospective produced three action items, all in early execution:

1. **DLR audit** (LA, due 2026-04-04): Full audit of all 35 DLRs to identify which reference ASM-0005, ASM-0007, or ASM-0012 and whether their conclusions remain valid under updated assumption parameters. Preliminary scan: 11 DLRs affected, 6 require financial re-projection, 3 require decision revision, 2 are informational-only impact.
2. **Assumption lifecycle framework** (LA, due 2026-04-15): Formal policy for assumption management including: (a) mandatory expiry dates with automated alerts at 14-day and 7-day horizons; (b) quarterly stress-test reviews where each active assumption is tested against an adversarial scenario; (c) cascade-impact maps -- every ASM record must declare which DLRs depend on it, and invalidation of an ASM triggers automatic review flags on all dependent DLRs. This framework will be codified as CANON-v1.4 Section 6.
3. **FY2026 reforecast** (Finance, due 2026-04-10): Updated operating plan using revised assumption parameters. Key changes: CCU ceiling raised to 4x (per DLR-20260306-001); churn-elasticity model replaced with state-dependent v3.0 (per DRIFT-20260318-001 resolution); competitive tolerance window revised from 72h to 18h. Preliminary reforecast shows FY2026 revenue at $452M (vs $480M plan, -5.8%) and EBITDA at $82M (vs $96M plan, -14.6%).

This drift will remain open until all three action items are completed and the reforecast is accepted by the board.
