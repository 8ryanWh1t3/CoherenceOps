## Signal

The 48-hour patch SLA defined in ASM-0003 ("Critical hotfix deployment within 48h of severity classification") was nearly breached on a minor client-side hotfix (build 4.1.7-hf2). The hotfix addressed a UI rendering glitch in the Season 4 Battle Pass preview screen that caused incorrect rarity indicators on 3 cosmetic items. The fix was classified as priority at 2026-02-26 15:00 UTC, and the patch was deployed at 2026-02-28 11:00 UTC -- 44 hours elapsed against a 48-hour SLA window.

The 4-hour buffer was the thinnest margin on any hotfix deployment since ASM-0003 was ratified 6 months ago. The previous tightest margin was 38 hours (12h buffer) on a server-side matchmaking fix in December 2025. The SLA has been met on all 23 hotfixes since adoption, but 44h represents a concerning trend.

Contributing factors included a 14-hour delay in QA sign-off (Berlin QA team was transitioning between shifts during a German public holiday on Feb 27), and a 6-hour delay in platform certification submission (Sony certification queue backed up due to a platform-wide submission surge ahead of Spring releases).

## Impact

- **Governance**: No actual SLA breach, but the near-miss triggers a mandatory review under ASM-0003 Section 4.2 ("margin analysis required when buffer < 6h"). This is the first time the margin analysis clause has been invoked.
- **Player experience**: The UI glitch was cosmetic-only, affecting ~120K players who viewed the Battle Pass preview screen between Feb 25-28. No gameplay impact. 47 support tickets filed.
- **Financial**: Negligible direct impact. The incorrect rarity indicators did not affect pricing or purchase flows.

## Root Cause

Two compounding delays eroded the SLA buffer:

1. **QA shift gap**: Berlin QA team handles overnight certification for NA-originated hotfixes. February 27 was a regional holiday (Weiberfastnacht, part of Carnival season), and the Berlin QA skeleton crew had a 14-hour turnaround vs. the normal 6-hour window. The holiday was not flagged in the release calendar.
2. **Platform certification queue**: Sony's certification queue had a 6-hour backlog (vs. normal 2h) due to a surge of Spring release submissions. AetherForge does not have expedited certification status with Sony (unlike Microsoft, where the turnaround was 1.5h).

## Cascade Chain

This drift did not cascade operationally but prompted a governance review of the SLA framework ahead of the high-stakes W10 tournament window.

- Upstream: UI rendering bug introduced in build 4.1.7 (Feb 24)
- Downstream: Triggered ASM-0003 Section 4.2 margin review
- Related: ASM-0003 (patch SLA assertion), DLR-20260228-001 (delivery review noting SLA margin concern)

## Patch / Resolution

1. **Immediate**: Hotfix 4.1.7-hf2 deployed successfully at 11:00 UTC Feb 28. UI rendering glitch resolved. Verified across all platforms (PC, PS5, Xbox Series, Switch).
2. **Process improvement** (Mar 1): Berlin studio updated the shared release calendar to include all regional holidays for 2026. QA coverage handoff protocol now requires 48h advance escalation when a holiday falls within a potential SLA window.
3. **Platform relations** (Mar 3): LA studio initiated discussions with Sony PlayStation Partners to explore expedited certification tier (requires $85K/yr commitment, pending CFO approval in Q2 budget review).
4. **SLA review**: ASM-0003 margin analysis completed. No SLA revision recommended at this time, but a "holiday buffer" clause was proposed for the next governance cycle.
